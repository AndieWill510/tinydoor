## Chapter 10: How Big is This Problem, Anyway?

- There are roughly 9 million Autistic people in the US alone

- There may be millions treated by ABA worldwide – but we need better
  data.

- There are 195,829 licensed ABA therapists in the US<sup>151</sup>

  - That’s bigger than the number of employees at Google (Alphabet).

  - Conversely, there are 182,030 Clinical Psychologists, Psychiatrists
    and LMFT’s -- or what most of us think of as
    therapists<sup>150</sup>

- If every Autistic child were in ABA (and thankfully, they’re not), on
  a per-child ratio basis, we have twice as many ABA technicians as
  schoolteachers

- 2/3 of ABA therapists (RBTs):

  - Require only a high school diploma, and a mere 40 hours of
    training<sup>152,153</sup>

  - Make on average less than fast food workers where I live,
    \$19.69/hour<sup>148</sup>

- Teachers, where I live<sup>154</sup>:

  - Average a master's degree and require *600* hours of training. As it
    should be.

  - Make an average of \$66,000 a year, owing to their advanced degree,
    and for the trust is placed in them for the impact they have on
    children.

- In the NHS in the UK (where I started my journey with this book), the
  only state-sanctioned early childhood intervention is ABA

- Same for Canada

- Consider that ABA is not really banned for Autistic people anywhere

Meanwhile, this is a multibillion-dollar franchise, which has your
children (or had/has you) in the hands of largely high school graduates
(or college graduates with a degree in *something*) with almost no
training and next to no supervision, certainly not in-session
supervision. Just like any franchise: the franchisee/corporation (ABA
clinics) rake in the dollars *because* of barely trained, low skilled,
low-income workers. Who are the customers? Parents, and oddly Medical
Insurance Companies. Who or what are the raw materials for the end
product being sold? By now it should be fairly obvious, but in case it’s
not: it’s kids. Not sick kids. Kids with a disability. They go into ABA
disabled. They come out of ABA *sick*. Fast food chain junior managers
get 160-320 hours of training, by franchise comparison. Again, entrusted
with far less responsibility and potential for harm, one could easily
argue. Who is it, again, that is doing all of this damage to our
Autistic kids? The worker bees (yes) or the franchisees (yes) or the
payor/insurance companies (yes) or the hapless legislators that
enshrined this in law (yes). All four are culpable. And all four are
incentivized and "win" but this truly is a fast food chain-like
franchise: the winners *win* at the cost of the long-term health of the
customers, who are constantly sold on the notion that this is the
*cheapest* alternative. Besides, and we all know by now, no franchise
*this* lucrative does so without tremendous harm to our world. Not
Exxon/Mobil, not Altria (cigarettes/vaping), and not predatory payday
lending companies. A license to print money. And all of us pay the
price. But no one pays the price heavier than the Autistic person who
went through ABA. Or the millions who were treated by a society
influenced by ABA philosophy.

So, what then? ABA is in general demonstrably, in some cases
irreparably, lethally harmful. It is scientifically proven in general to
*cause* varying degrees of mental and physical illnesses. How do we
unwind this nightmare? There is hope. There is another option than
merely living through it.
