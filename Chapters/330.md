There is a relatively new and very offensive saying that “we’re all a little Autistic.” Similarly, Masters and Johnson in the 1960’s might have been summarized by saying “we’re all a little queer.” The answer to both is, well, no. You either are experiencing what it is to be in the LGBTQIA+ or QTBIPOC, day in and day out, or you are not. I have lived my adult life as a heteronormative white male and I lay no claim to understanding the struggles you face on the daily. That said, there are many Autistic people out there who seek your company, your fellowship, and offer not only resources but real connection.

I cannot vouch for each of these resources, but I hope that in some small way that this offering is accepted in the way it is made – with a genuine human recognition that we all need to find our tribe. And with a percentage well into the double digits in terms of the number of Autistic people who are LGBTQIA+ and QTBIPOC, I hope that you find a great tribe and one that offers you much more than I have in the pages above.

There is also a silent epidemic amongst LGBTQIA+ Autistic people which is sexual abuse. It is very important that Autistic people, starting in pre-adolescence, get the facts regarding sexual health and relationship boundaries. A great resource for learning the boundaries of healthy relationships is the series of podcasts here: https://researchautism.org/self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates-podcast/ And, it is very important that Autistic people have a healthy relationship with sex, which starts with regular self-pleasure or masturbation. Orgasms release oxytocin, which in addition to counteracting cortisol (a stress hormone), can also counteract some of the effects of toxic stress, also helps us connect with our bodies in a way that promotes somatic mindfulness208. This was discussed during the Stimming Chapter, and because the intersection of sex, LGBTQIA+ and Autism carries such a strong taboo it’s critical that LGBTQIA+ Autistic people connect early with their bodies in a healthy, pleasurable way.
