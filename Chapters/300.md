Let’s start with a few definitions. We’ll take Autistic Meltdown, Sleep Deprivation, Shutdown, Burn(out) and each one in turn. For each, we will:

Define each in terms of a variety of behaviors

Talk about what each one is not

Talk briefly about the underlying conditions

Talk about contributing factors (not causes)

I think, regardless of the layer (meltdown, sleep deprivation, shutdown, or burn(out)), you would be well-served by treating these like any other illnesses:

Get plenty of rest (beyond just regular sleep), such as a brief nap or so a day and time away from social media

Make sure and stay hydrated (which is water, yes, but also hydration salts), not too much sodium or caffeine

Good nutritious food which balances what makes you happy with balanced diet

See an Autistic-affirming doctor and therapist

Talk daily/weekly with your Autistic Coach to stay on track

### Meltdown

Meltdown can be any one of the following:

Intense emotional outbursts, such as crying, yelling, or lashing out³⁴

Physical symptoms, such as biting, hitting, kicking, self-injury, or throwing and breaking objects¹²

Difficulty communicating or processing information, such as inability to talk, lack of ability to think, or wanting to escape²⁴

Withdrawal such as rocking, hiding, or eloping¹²⁵

Autistic meltdowns are not:

Temper tantrums

A form of manipulation

A complete and immediate withdrawal from a situation or environment where you cannot do anything but lie down (that is shutdown)

A long-term low-energy event spanning weeks or months (that is burn(out))

Autistic meltdowns:

Are a “pain” signal that your needs have not been met and are now seriously ill and in need of first aid:

treat it as you would a severe sunburn with comfort and kindness

after you feel better do your best to understand which of your needs were not met (there likely were several)

do what you can to not put yourself in that situation again (pruning), just like you would after a sunburn

Are a genuine cry of distress due to sensory or emotional overload¹²

Can happen at any age and vary in intensity and duration²⁴

Are often preceded by signs of distress or anxiety, such as stimming or eloping¹²

Can happen after a shutdown

Can happen while you are in burn(out)

### Autistic Sleep Deprivation

Sleep Deprivation can be any one of the following:

Feeling drowsy, tired, or fatigued during the day³⁴

Having trouble concentrating, remembering, or making decisions¹²³⁴

Experiencing mood changes, such as irritability, anxiety, or depression¹²⁴

Having a reduced sex drive or performance²⁴

Being more prone to infections or illnesses¹³

Sleep deprivation is not:

Something to be taken lightly; it is a symptom of one or more needs not being met

Specific to Autism (but it is common)

The only cause of Meltdowns, Shutdowns and Burn(out) – but they are a contributing factor and in fact can be a cyclical symptom of Burn(out) and/or Depression

Sleep deprivation:

Is “rocket fuel” for any number of mental and physical illnesses – but specifically it amplifies meltdown, shutdown and burnout

Is a “pain” signal that your needs have not been met and are now seriously ill and in need of first aid:

treat it as you would “heat exhaustion” with comfort and kindness

after you feel better do your best to understand which of your needs were not met (there likely were several)

do what you can to not put yourself in that situation again (pruning), just like you would after severe heat exposure

strongly recommend that you start DBT/ACT therapy to learn how to prevent these issue in the future

Can affect your physical health and appearance

Weight gain

Dark circles under the eyes

Premature aging of the skin²

Can increase the risk of accidents or injuries due to impaired reaction times or judgment¹² which is a major cause of death in Autistic people

Be a form of domestic abuse, if your abuser is not allowing you to sleep adequately – putting your health as an Autistic person in greater jeopardyz2

Make meltdown, shutdown and burn(out) more likely. Why/how:

Sleep deprivation can impair your judgment, emotional control, and mental flexibility, which can make you more prone to act impulsively or irrationallyz³⁴.

Sleep deprivation can increase your negative emotions, such as fatigue, anxiety, and depression, which can make you more irritable or aggressivez¹²³⁴.

Sleep deprivation can cause physical discomfort and pain, such as headaches, stomach aches, and flu-like symptoms, which can make you more sensitive to triggers or threatsz¹².

### Shutdown

Shutdown is straightforward, even if the solution is not:

Difficulty (impossibility in) communicating or processing information, such as inability to talk, lack of ability to think, and

Needing (not wanting) to escape

Withdrawal such as eloping

Autistic shutdown is not:

Lashing out or sitting in a situation crying or rocking (that’s meltdown)

A long-term low-energy event spanning weeks or months (that is burn(out))

Autistic shutdowns:

Are a “pain” signal that your needs have not been met and are now gravely ill and in need of first aid:

treat it as you would heat stroke and tend to your needs immediately (you likely will have no other choice, anyway)

after you feel better it is critical that you understand which of your needs were not met (there likely were several)

do everything in you power to not put yourself in that situation again (pruning), just like you would after a heat stroke – this is a very serious health condition that needs immediate medical attention

Are a warning sign of distress and impending much worse illness due to intense dysregulation, sensory overload and likely other contributing factors such as abuse or bullying/shunning/gaslighting

May be due to a type of masking that is more severe: compensating and assimilating – look to rid yourself of these habits/adaptations and the environments that drove them to be in the first place

Can happen at any age and yet the intensity and duration may be similar

Are often preceded by signs of distress or anxiety, such as stimming or eloping¹²

Can happen after a meltdown

Can happen while you are in burn(out)

### Burn(out)

Burn(out), like physical burns to your body, is multilayered:

Depending upon the expert, there are 4-6 levels of Burn(out) – I will be using 5 levels as that is how I was introduced to the topic (by Matthew Lawrence). Where I got this right, it is thanks to him, where I may have omitted symptoms, it is because of me.

In general, burn(out) is characterized by:

Discomfort

Migraine

Brain Fog

Emotional lability

Skill/ability loss

Impaired motor skills and athletic ability

Communication difficulties (expressive aphasia, word search loss, incorrect language word or phrase substitutions)

Stimming more

Get sick more often

Sickness lasts longer

Digestion issues are more common

Here are the levels of Burn(out). It’s helpful to think of Burn(out) not as “Burnout” but of “burns” as in first degree through fourth degree burns. This is not mild depression or job burnout like what we might see with NT people. Treat this more like a series of skin/body burns and that the deeper the level the more life-threatening it will become:

Level 1: You are chronically exhausted. You must lie down at the end of your day and you are unable to function as well as you normally do – a good night’s rest and you are ready to go. You mask more, and you are doing “dopamine seeking” behavior.

Level 2: All of Level 1. You must lie down at the end of your day and you are unable to function as well as you normally do – it takes a full day to recover from the day before. You can notice that your skills are impaired, including executive function. This is in the normal range of dysfunction for you, “I’ve seen this before.” You can mask less, and you are doing more “dopamine seeking” behavior.

Level 3: All of Level 2. You cannot focus or complete tasks as before. Sensory triggers are stronger than they normally are. It takes multiple days to recover. You need to work shorter days and cannot work a full week of days. Your executive function/skill level are not in a normal range. People are starting to notice. Meltdowns and shutdowns are more frequent.

Level 4: All of Level 3. Most people recognize Burn(out) at this stage or Level 5. You may need to take a leave of absence. Sensory triggers are more extreme to the point of being debilitating. Executive function and other skills are widely noticed by others. “You seem more Autistic,” others may say. You are having mental health issues such as anxiety disorders. Health care providers (doctors, therapists) are recognizing you, as you are regularly in their offices seeking medical attention.

Level 5: All of Level 4. You are permanently in bed. You are having not only mental health but physical health symptoms. Anything can cause a meltdown or shutdown, and seemingly all the time. Sensory triggers don’t stop. You are living in shutdown. Thoughts of MD, up to and including making plans are common.

Autistic burn(out) is not:

The same thing as job burnout or garden-variety depression. This is a serious medical condition, akin to a Major Depressive Disorder, that requires immediate medical and therapeutic intervention.

Only lashing out or sitting in a situation crying or rocking (that’s meltdown). Note that meltdown can occur during burn(out).

Only immediate withdrawal from a situation or environment where you cannot do anything but lie down (that is shutdown). Note that shutdown can occur during burn(out).

Autistic burn(out):

Is a “pain” signal that your needs have not been met and are now gravely ill and in need of immediate changes to your environment to save your life:

treat it as you would skin/body burns and prune anything that is preventing you from getting your needs met immediately (you likely will feel that you have a choice, but believe me this is an illusion)

you may have moments of energy, and during these times it is critical that you understand which of your needs were not met (there likely were several) and take immediate corrective action

do everything in you power to not put yourself in that situation again (pruning), just like you would after a severe burn from a fire (not like a sunburn)

Is a clear symptom of illness and impending much worse illness due to intense dysregulation, sensory overload and likely other contributing factors such as abuse or bullying/shunning/gaslighting

Is likely due to more severe forms of masking: compensating and assimilating – for your survival it is critical that rid yourself of these habits/adaptations and the environments that drove them to be in the first place – and this happens most effectively through therapy like DBT and ACT

Can happen at any age and yet the intensity and duration may be similar

Are often preceded by signs of distress or anxiety, such as shutdowns
