## Chapter 18: How the Cycles Influence Each Other

Bullying

<img src="media/image7.png" style="width:6.57511in;height:7in" />

So, remember that SOURCE is the foundation of autistiphobia, no matter
how it manifests itself; in the spectrum of those who don’t know us to
those who know us best. If we tie this together, the Stigma Cycle of
autistiphobia results in bullying that kicks off the Autistic Protective
Mimicry Cycle. The Grief Cycle of autistiphobia reinforces the Mimicry
Cycle, in that it invalidates the lived experience of Autistic people as
they deal with the Stigma Cycle of bullies. This is not all that the
Grief Cycle invalidates, in the same way that the Stigma Cycle drives
isolation in more ways than simple bullying. Through acceptance and
reward, the bullying that happens to Autistic people also:

1.  Gives “permission” to other people to act as bullies to Autistic
    people

2.  Reinforces shunning behavior of non-bullies (who greatly outnumber
    bullies)

The net-effect is widespread social isolation. Because of the messages
received through the Grief Cycle that validate the Stigma Cycle (“go
along to get along,” “don’t bother a bully they won’t bother you,” “you
must learn to get along with people you don’t like”), the Mimicry Cycle
is accelerated faster and deeper. This drives not only isolation but
further bullying and shunning; to the point where living becomes
traumatic. Because Autistic people only have the social lessons taught
by others to lean on, they double and triple-down, driving Masking into
a deeper level in Compensation and then deeper levels still in
Assimilation. The Mimicry Cycle spins faster and faster until Autistic
Burn(out) levels reach first-degree/second-degree burns and if not
treated, deeper and deeper it can go, until the Protective Mimicry Cycle
is no longer Protective – it's Destructive and, in some cases, lethal.
But remember – this started by being around bullies and shunners and was
reinforced by people living in grief. It was caused by exposure: it’s
reversable.

Shunning

<img src="media/image8.png" style="width:6.57511in;height:7in" />

Shunning is a far bigger problem, because there are a lot more shunners
than bullies. We need good data to know the exact ratio. Again, SOURCE
is the foundation of autistiphobia, no matter how it manifests itself;
in the spectrum of those who don’t know us to those who know us best. If
we tie this together, the Stigma Cycle of autistiphobia results in
shunning that kicks off the Autistic Protective Mimicry Cycle. The Grief
Cycle of autistiphobia reinforces the Mimicry Cycle, in that it
invalidates the lived experience of Autistic people as they deal with
the Stigma Cycle of shunners. This is not all that the Grief Cycle
invalidates, in the same way that the Stigma Cycle drives isolation in
more ways than simple bullying. Through acceptance and reward, the
shunning that happens to Autistic people also

1.  Gives “permission” to other people to shun Autistic people

2.  Reinforces bullying behavior through identifying socially “isolated”
    people

The net-effect is deep social isolation. Because of the messages
received through the Grief Cycle that validate the Stigma Cycle (“go
along to get along,” “you must learn to get along with people you don’t
like”), the Mimicry Cycle is accelerated faster and deeper. This drives
not only isolation but further bullying and shunning; to the point where
living becomes traumatic. Because Autistic people only have the social
lessons taught by others to lean on, they double and triple-down,
driving Masking into a deeper level in Compensation and then deeper
levels still in Assimilation. The Mimicry Cycle spins faster and faster
until Autistic Burn(out) levels reach first-degree/second-degree burns
and if not treated, deeper and deeper it can go, until the Protective
Mimicry Cycle is no longer Protective – it's Destructive and, in some
cases, lethal. But remember – this started by being around bullies and
shunners and was reinforced by people living in grief. It was caused by
exposure: it’s reversable.

So, let’s take a step back and look at these cycles in a different way,
through a comparative table:

| (Protective /Destructive) Mimicry | Mimic      |     | Grief       | Autistiphobia |
|-----------------------------------|------------|-----|-------------|---------------|
| Masking                           | Camouflage |     | Denial      | Blame         |
| Compensating                      | Masquerade |     | Anger       | Shame         |
| Meltdown                          | Rewarding  |     | Bargaining  | Rage          |
| Assimilating                      | Batesian   |     | Depression  | Bully/Shun    |
| Shutdown                          | Crypsis    |     | Resignation | Reward        |
| Burn(out)                         | Necrosis   |     | Habituation | Habituation   |

So, at the end of the Mimic cycle is not further Crypsis/Burn(out) but
Necrosis. We think of necrotic tissue in the body as tissue that has
died or is dying. It’s the same thing with Autistic Burnout. If not
addressed, the Destructive Mimicry Cycle in late burnout can cross over
from a retreat/isolation/avoidance/loss of skills into a desire for
death, to relieve what feels like the only option – *but it isn’t the
only option*.
