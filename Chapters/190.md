## Chapter 19: Treating Burnout

Burnout is at ground-level a trauma response, and there are good
therapies for trauma recovery (EMDR<sup>170</sup> for example), but it
requires sacrifice and a willingness to let go of those parts of your
life or people in your life that are driving you further and deeper into
Autistic Burnout. It will also likely require therapy, long term, to
help you to identify your core values and bring your life into better
alignment (see Acceptance and Commitment Therapy or ACT<sup>171</sup>,
diagram below). Identifying and being mindful of your feelings and
experiences can be helped through either Dialectical Behavior Therapy or
DBT<sup>172</sup> and/or Somatic Therapy<sup>173,174</sup>. There is an
alternative to going into and staying in Autistic Burnout. It is not
inevitable or normal, and you don’t have to live like this! You have
another option. You have *many* options to a life spent feeling like
this<sup>174</sup>. The good news of burnout is that so many things
contribute and anything you do will help. Here are some good places to
start:
