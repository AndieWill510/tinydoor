## Chapter 12: The Costs of Improbably High Social Standards

Shifting gears, I want to do another loop of Five Whys, this time on NT
social standards. I believe that Autistic experience comes out of a
reaction to these improbably high social standards, so they bear deeper
examination. By understanding them only then can we start to develop
countermeasures:

- Society enforces improbable standards of behavior on people, and then
  creates environments that distract them from seeing it for what it is:
  an unwinnable game. Why?

- To create stigma/shame. Why?

- Self-stigmatized people are easier to control. Why?

- Fear of isolation/detachment from love is a powerful – perhaps the
  most powerful – motivator in humans. Why?

- Humans evolved to need social interaction to attain and maintain
  homeostasis: in short, social interactions evolved to keep us alive
  and healthy.

In society, people with power promote improbably high social standards
of behavior, then use stigma and distraction as a cudgel, to maintain
power. For NT people, this distraction keeps them largely unaware of
this unwinnable game and how much power is being siphoned from them
through a loss of agency. For Autistic people, however, this is not
distraction: it is overstimulation and a pathway to disability and
ultimately, illness.

People are distracted into self-stigmatization and do all in their power
to adhere to impossible social standards. They also often do all in
their power to drive others to meet these impossible social standards
because “that’s the way it is.” This phrase is an example of
self-reinforcing delusional logic if ever there was any.
Self-stigmatized people then drive stigma in others through:

1.  Shunning; the threat of isolation

2.  Bullying; intimidation or threat of abuse

3.  Gaslighting; a type of abuse (crazy making) designed to silence
    dissent by making the person doubt their own judgment, beliefs and
    perceptions

Our society consists of a series of environments, built to keep NT
people from “acting out” against improbable social standards as follows:

- School classrooms distract and at times entertain

- Constant meetings in the workplace drive better collaboration and
  ideally, task productivity

- Gambling casinos excite the NT adult to be more willing to gamble and
  lose money

- Supermarkets, like casinos, are an artificially lighted and sounding
  environment meant to drive more consumption

However, these environments that distract and manipulate the NT mind are
for an Autistic mind overwhelming and overstimulating. It is in the
overloading of the Autistic sensory system that are at the root of
“inappropriate” social interactions and behaviors. Autistic people can
be forgiven for seeing the world as if it was intentionally designed to
dysregulate them; and once dysregulated, punished through:

1.  Shunning

2.  Bullying

3.  Gaslighting

This is the ultimate double-bind, and it drives Autistic people into
deeper dysregulation, illness and trauma.
