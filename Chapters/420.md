Feelings of isolation and shame can have significant impacts on a person’s health, both physically and mentally:

- Mental Health: Isolation and shame can lead to increased risk for mental health disorders such as depression and anxiety12. They can also lead to changes in brain function, antisocial behavior, and a higher risk for suicide1.

- Physical Health: These feelings can contribute to poorer physical health, including conditions such as heart disease and high blood pressure1. They can also lead to increased alcohol and substance use1.

- Behavioral Changes: Isolation can lead to avoidance of social interactions, including those that were once enjoyable2. It can also result in distress during periods of solitude and dread associated with social activities2.

- Emotional Impact: Isolation and shame can cause individuals to become emotionally numb, detached from their own feelings2. This emotional isolation can further exacerbate feelings of loneliness and depression2.

- Self-Esteem: Feelings of isolation and shame can lead to low self-esteem, which can further impact mental health2.

- Stress Levels: Isolation can increase stress levels, which can have various negative impacts on both physical and mental health1.

Remember that these are general impacts and the specific effects can vary from person to person.

Cortisol is a hormone that helps the body respond to stress. However, too much cortisol can have several physical and emotional consequences:

Physical Consequences:

Weight Gain: High cortisol levels can cause weight gain, especially around the midsection and upper back12.

Skin Changes: It can lead to thinning skin, easy bruising, and acne2.

Muscle Weakness: Too much cortisol can result in muscle weakness3.

High Blood Pressure: Elevated cortisol levels can lead to high blood pressure2.

Digestive Problems: High cortisol levels can disrupt digestion4.

Emotional Consequences:

Depression and Anxiety: High levels of cortisol are associated with depression and anxiety1.

Fatigue: Too much cortisol can lead to severe fatigue2.

Irritability: High cortisol levels can cause irritability2.

Difficulty Concentrating: Elevated cortisol levels can make it difficult to concentrate2.

It’s important to note that long-term exposure to high levels of cortisol can disrupt almost all of the body’s processes, increasing the risk of many health problems, including heart disease, lung issues, obesity, anxiety, depression, and more24. If you’re experiencing symptoms of high cortisol levels, it’s recommended to consult with a healthcare professional.
