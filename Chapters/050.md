## Chapter 5: So How Did Stimming (and Sexuality) Become Such a Stigma?

Society, parents, doctors are led into Autistic awareness,
philosophically, by a small army of Autistic Applied Behavioral (ABA)
technicians who are dedicated to force Autistic people to stop stimming.
I find that language matters, and ABA technicians, per all available
evidence I could find, are not providing “therapy” in the medical sense
of treatment and healing you might receive from a Clinical Psychologist
or Psychiatrist.

The Oxford English Dictionary defines “therapy” as:

> "The medical treatment of disease; curative medical or psychiatric
> treatment."

There are at least two problems here, as I see it:

1.  Autistic adults, Autistic advocacy groups and Autistic-participatory
    research organizations<sup>34</sup> reject the Pathological Model of
    Autism, which means that Autism is a disability not a
    disease/disorder. If there’s no disease, then no cure is possible
    because it’s not required. So, this can’t be *therapy* per the first
    part of Oxford’s definition. If there’s no therapy happening, then
    the people offering the work can’t rightly be called *therapists*.
    I’ll use the word ABA *technician*, and I invite you to do the same.

2.  Even if you buy into the Pathological Model of Autism, which I and
    many other Autistic adults and researchers do not, ABA as a modality
    must be *curative*. However, ABA never sought to *cure* Autism, and
    on this it was very clear even from the very historical beginnings.
    It sought instead to change, or mask, the *behavioral appearance* of
    Autism while leaving all of the non-behavioral characteristics in
    place. Behavior is the figurative tip of the iceberg: the vast
    majority of what we are as people, Autistic and otherwise, lies
    beneath the waterline. ABA technicians and researchers not only
    didn’t acknowledge the majority of processes that make us human –
    for them they simply didn’t matter. So, virtually none of what makes
    an Autistic person *a person* is addressed in ABA apart from one
    arguably trivial (but obvious) part: the behavior. We are more than
    the behavior of stimming or avoidance or awkward social
    interactions, there is a universe of experiences and processes below
    the behavioral surface of a person. So again, no cure, no therapy.
    No therapy, no therapists. So, we’re left with ABA *technicians*.

So, ABA technicians may call it *therapy* and they may be called (and
call themselves) *therapists*, their training is strictly technical
work, akin to training a mouse to push a button in a box. The closest
clinical modality (to ABA for Autism) is “gay conversion ‘therapy’,” and
has been outlawed in 22 US States<sup>37</sup>, Canada, France, Greece
and New Zealand and the Australian State of Victoria<sup>38,39</sup>,
with many more on the way. Remember that “gay conversion therapy” also
originated with Ivar Lovaas at UCLA, run in a program side-by-side with
ABA for Autistic kids<sup>52</sup>. When you start with the same origin,
the same modality, *the very same researcher/progenitor* you get the
same impact and of course the same results. So instead of therapy we’ll
use the phrase “ABA indoctrination." There are worse words we can
choose, and others have, but *indoctrination* fits. Again from the
Oxford English Dictionary, “indoctrination” is defined as:

> “instruction; formal teaching. Also spec., the ‘instruction’ of
> prisoners of war, etc., in …doctrines, ideas, etc.; = **brainwashing**
> \[bold, mine\], n.”

Also, through something euphemistically called "aversives," many
Autistic patients are indoctrinated through ABA to not stim in
overwhelming environments, as well as a range of random behaviors *that
don’t fit <u>the opinion</u>* of the ABA technician at that time. It’s
an old adage in medicine, "beware the cure that’s worse than the
disease" (worse still, is a cure in search of disease *that isn’t one*).
Regardless of disease or disability, this idea of treatment is so
important that doctors enshrined an entire ethical credo around it: The
Hippocratic Oath, "do no harm." However, Hippocrates is spinning in his
proverbial grave on the part of this ½ Trillion-dollar industry
delivering a cure worse than the disability. Because, in our case, you
have an entire industry researching and delivering ever-evolving
“treatments” for something that *isn’t* a disease. Think of the damage
that would happen to you if you had a course of chemotherapy or
radiation when you don’t have cancer. The impact of ABA, as we learned
with “gay conversion therapy” is that the impact of the indoctrination
is worse than the behavior, e.g., stimming. In fact, ABA is
indoctrination *designed to stop the built-in therapy* which is
stimming. There is an old saying: "The road to hell is paved with good
intentions." Except, as an Autistic person, this approach has made
*your* life hell: a life where you will not only be overwhelmed, but
*you can’t stim – I would go so far as say you can’t express yourself in
any way physically, including sexually*. No pathway to joy. No relief
from overwhelming stressors. A pressure cooker with no release valve. A
proverbial ticking time-bomb. So, what’s the solution to this double
bind?

Rule \#1: There can be no cure for something nature built you to do:
stimming. <span class="mark">Stimming doesn’t require therapy, *<u>it is
therapy</u>*</span>.

Rule \#2: I promise you; we’re not stimming enough. Sensory
overstimulation or understimulation creates a “pressure cooker,”
relieved through Joyful Stimming.

Rule \#3: If you don’t find ways to regularly stim, and that includes
masturbation and other forms of healthy sexual expression, your body
will get sick. No joy = no health.

Rule \#4: Make it your mission: stim as much as you want however and
<u>wherever you safely can</u>.

Corollary \#1: It’s not always safe to stim like you want to or are used
to or where you want to or are used to<sup>53</sup>. Fact.

Corollary \#2: This is like a role-playing game like D&D. You need other
Actually Autistic people ("your squad") to not only learn how to stim
(when you can’t stim), but to stim in a safe way and a safe
place<sup>53</sup>. See Rule \#3. Learn about stealth stimming, and
low-distraction stim toys as alternatives<sup>54</sup>.

We’re going to talk much more about undoing some of the ABA "cure"
damage I talked about. And no, you don’t have to have gone through years
of ABA indoctrination to wind up like this. Society, over your childhood
and early adulthood, will do its own version of ABA through peer
pressure alone – no direct ABA indoctrination required. If you grew up
in a society permeated by ABA (e.g., the US/North America, parts of
Europe), you got indoctrinated by ABA, indirectly. We’ll talk more about
this in Appendix A (trigger warning) and how this could be possible.

There are really three "cure" damages here:

1.  First is that we’ve been trained not to stim. That toxic,
    life-shortening advice, is in fact a form of psychological and in
    many cases physical abuse meant to make you socially "fit in."
    Although well-intended, it’s putting us right now in the closest
    version of hell possible on earth. Yet, now we hold the keys to get
    out. At least a little. Start slow. But do it: stim more and more.
    Your body will thank you. Joy is vital. It’s the fiber to the
    apple’s sugar.

2.  The second damage is that you were likely taught to hate (dislike?)
    yourself for doing what nature made you to do. The people who did
    this to you were likely not evil. But the actions they took without
    question were cruel, and because it is putting you through a second
    hell. Here’s the good news! You now have a choice: continue with
    what they trained you to do and get sicker or spend more time around
    people who will accept you for how nature created you. It really is
    your choice. Choose wisely: you deserve better.

3.  Third, you have been and likely still are going through aversives
    even if you’re not going through ABA. Being placed, or placing
    yourself, in sensory-overwhelming environments for extended periods
    of time without adequate breaks/relief is not healthy for you, more
    likely than not. Although some experts refer to this as "exposure
    therapy," and others may even recommend it for you, there is a more
    accurate term: “trauma<sup>55</sup>.” And it I believe is at root
    cause for something we see a lot of with adult Autistic people:
    Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder or PTSD, and just as often in
    Autistic people it’s Complex PTSD (C-PTSD). Or it could also be a
    condition known in the Neurodivergent Community as Rejection
    Sensitive Dysphoria or RSD<sup>32</sup>. And C-PTSD and RSD are not
    mutually exclusive. You could have both. Note that The Diagnostic
    and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM) does not currently
    acknowledge RSD as a recognized disorder. It’s OK, and this isn’t
    the first time that the DSM is late to the party, but they’ll catch
    up. I have a section on the DSM and a brief history of Autism at the
    end of the book. But, to better understand the dynamic of
    environmental overstimulation/understimulation and the oppression of
    stimming – leading to C-PTSD/RSD, we need more data.

As painter Bob Ross, Great Autistic Coach, used to say: "You can do
anything you want. This is your world."<sup>41</sup>
