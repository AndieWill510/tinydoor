For some Schizophrenia is a neurotype. For others, it’s a disorder. Unlike Bipolar, I would strongly encourage you to find the best possible psychiatrist to treat this grave mental illness. Bipolar can result in psychosis. Schizophrenia is in part defined by psychosis. This is not to be treated lightly.

Schizophrenia can be a major contributing factor to not only emotional dysregulation in an Autistic person’s life, but their physical safety.

I’ve said elsewhere that there are great psychiatrists and there are bad ones. You need referrals (preferably several) from people you know and/or doctors you trust to get to a great one. Fire the bad ones fast: schizophrenia is a grave mental illness and must be treated with medication. Also, get stable as fast as you can and stay compliant with your medication. Yes, the side effects are not pleasant, but think of the alternative: you being physically unsafe in the street. Get regular sleep and regular exercise. This is all part of how you will stay well. Stay well. You are worth it. Your life very likely depends upon you staying on your meds and being compliant with your psychiatrist (doctor’s) orders.

There is also a silent epidemic amongst Autistic people which is sexual abuse. It is very important that Autistic people, starting in pre-adolescence, get the facts regarding sexual health and relationship boundaries. A great resource for learning the boundaries of healthy relationships is the series of podcasts here: https://researchautism.org/self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates-podcast/ And, it is very important that Autistic people have a healthy relationship with sex, which starts with regular self-pleasure or masturbation. Orgasms release oxytocin, which in addition to counteracting cortisol (a stress hormone), can also counteract some of the effects of toxic stress, also helps us connect with our bodies in a way that promotes somatic mindfulness208. People who have both Autism and Schizophrenia may carry extra stress, so that natural release is perhaps even more important than for other Autistic people. This was discussed during the Stimming Chapter, and because the intersection of sex and Autism carries such a strong taboo it’s critical that Autistic people connect early with their bodies in a healthy, pleasurable way.
