## Chapter 15: Internalized Ableism – The End of the Protective Mimicry Line

Protective mimicry, of which we as animals (humans) have several layers,
was designed to work in a short period of time. Either we got eaten or
it worked, and the danger passed and on we went with our merry lives
until the next discrete predatory encounter. But what if our system,
which is primed to deal with predators, is constantly reminded that
“this is a predator” (bully), *but it isn’t.* In the world of bullies
and shunners and gaslighters, these are not predators (that you bring
with you in your head): the net-effect is that these characters work
together to function as a parasitic infection. Please note
<span class="mark">I am not saying people are parasites or
predators</span>, but this is only an analogy or thought-exercise to how
they <u>may</u> impact the body. In the Autistic community we often talk
about “internalized ableism” – these are one and the same. I think that
the parasitic (thought) model helps expand and deepen our understanding
of internalized ableism. With each encounter with ableism, we suffer and
get sicker and sicker because we react out of and internalize a
situation with the *only option presented*.

Here’s what we often hear, and how it plays out:

| Advice Autistics Hear                                                                                                      | What we do                               |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| “Have a bully? Just ignore him.”                                                                                           | Camouflage/Masking                       |
| “If you don’t do anything to them, they won’t do anything to you.”                                                         | Masquerade/Compensation                  |
| “Bullies love isolated people, make friends with their friends and they leave you alone. They might even become a friend.” | Rewarding/Assimilation                   |
| “First thing you do, walk right up to a bully and punch them in the nose.”                                                 | Batesian/Meltdown                        |
| \<\<when none of the above works, and it rarely does\>\> -- Play “dead” short-term, withdraw, isolate.                     | Crypsis/Shutdown                         |
| \<\<when none of that works, and it rarely does\>\> -- Play extended version of “dead” which can become toxic and deadly.  | Crypsis(?) -- perhaps “Necrosis”/Burnout |

None of these strategies work, because there is something in the way
that Autistic people take well-intentioned NT advice about bullies
(analogy: predators) and apply it. The bullying does not stop. The
reason the bullying does not stop, or at least doesn’t stop for long
(for there is always another bully or bullying influence) is that these
are not bullies in the NT sense. They are not, strictly speaking,
classic (analogously) predators. They are, to us, effectively
(analogously) parasites, as their messages act as if a parasitic
infection because of the way we now think from their actions. Again, we
aren’t calling people parasites: we are calling what happens inside of
us (internalized ableism) a process highly similar to a parasitic immune
response.

Why do I say “parasite?” As a proof point, here are the symptoms in the
human body when you have a parasitic infection:

- Muscle aches (Fibromyalgia?)

- Fatigue (constant exhaustion?)

- Nausea, possibly vomiting (“stomach/GI problems”?)

- Diarrhea and other gastrointestinal distress (IBS?)

- Neurological symptoms like seizures, severe headaches, or
  disorientation (brain fog?)

- Skin symptoms, like redness, itching, rash or sores (eczema or
  psoriasis? maybe something you might “pick at” in a stim)

Some of this may be wholly unfamiliar to you. Others, you might see a
pattern. But GI problems, neurological symptoms, fatigue, muscle aches
and skin symptoms are all very common in Autism. Are these “symptoms” of
Autism? Or are we having a very, very normal (analogously) parasitic
infection immune response – the so-called true costs of internalized
ableism? This is one of those areas where we need more data to know for
certain, this is a theory that fits the facts. But what if, *what if*,
our mind was reacting to a predator in a classic Protective Mimicry
response, having zero impact but doing it repeatedly, meanwhile the body
was <u>at the same time</u> having a Protective *Parasitic* response. Is
there zero cost, then, to the evolutionary response of Protective
Mimicry – designed to protect us against a predator – when there’s not a
predator as such? If we keep doing the same thing repeatedly (protective
mimicry) expecting a different result, apologies to Einstein, but I
think for Autistic people it’s not *the definition of* “insanity” – I
believe it is a cause (or *the cause*) of severe and worsening forms of
mental (and psychosomatic/physical) illness. I believe this is ground
truth – or as we sometimes hear “ground zero” for where a lot of
Autistic people start to get quite ill. Our environment and evolution
have taught Autistic people to respond to a predator when what we really
have is a parasite (parasitoid), a parasitic infection. And, since our
mind/body system isn’t sure what we’re dealing with, instead of picking
one strategy, we’re responding to both (analogously) predator and
(analogously) parasitoid simultaneously. And, increasingly, it’s making
us physically ill and, in some cases, killing us. But what if it didn’t
have to? What if we could develop a defense, through knowledge and
practice, just like we can avoid leeches in a dirty pond?
