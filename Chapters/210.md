## Chapter 21: The Source of Dysregulation

I’ve talked about a Protective Mimicry System gone awry, or
dysregulated, so it becomes a Destructive Mimicry System. I’ve talked
about another environmentally caused dysregulation, the Immune response
to Parasites. Together, these form two points of dysregulation: Autistic
Predator-Parasite Defense Model. This defense starts with protective
intent and over time and with ineffectiveness becomes destructive and
causes illness. We can think of illness as a dysregulation of the body.
There are many forms: Mimicry and Immune are but two. Every Autistic
person over a certain age will recognize another form of dysregulation:
emotional. Emotional Dysregulation is at the heart of the Autistic
Meltdown, Shutdown, and Burnout. It’s also common for several
neurological diseases, but we’ll cover that Dysregulation in a moment.

Here are a few more:

- Perceptual Dysregulation

  - Actually Autistic Coach Matthew Lawrence has rightly pointed out
    that Sensory Integration challenges drive (Emotional) Dysregulation
    and vice versa.

  - Sensory integration challenges are not stable, they depend upon
    situation, scenario and such.

  - They are, therefore, themselves a dysregulation. So, we’ll add
    Perceptual (sensory integration) Dysregulation to the list.

  - There is no start to this list. Someone might ask, what is the
    starting dysregulation in Autistic people, the beginning of it? It’s
    akin to asking where the start of the world is on a map. There is no
    real “start,” however we are all born somewhere, so for you alone
    quite literally there is a start. It may be like that with Autistic
    Dysregulation: we all have our own individual starting point(s). But
    if I had to pick a place to start, I’d start with Perceptual
    Dysregulation. And work on that first, last and always.

- Next, only because I think of it this way, is Emotional Dysregulation,
  which we discussed a moment ago.

  - There is no regulation without emotional regulation, and you cannot
    stop dysregulation of the mind/body without regulation of emotions.

  - Mindfulness practices, such as DBT therapy and Buddhist meditation,
    help “ground” the body in the here and now. Grounding is a key
    practice in mindfulness and regulation, which is one of the reasons
    I believe DIR<sup>®</sup>/Floortime, play therapy and music therapy
    for Autistic kids works as well as they do. Loved ones literally
    “ground” alongside the Autistic child. Brilliant.

  - Emotional regulation is a practice, and it is very, very difficult
    if multiple dysregulations, especially Perceptual, are also
    happening at the same time.

- Next is Cognitive Dysregulation.

  - For people with bipolar or schizophrenia, they may recognize this as
    psychosis, depression or mania.

  - For people with severe depression, this may be cognitive distortions
    which feel very, very accurate but may be influenced by brain
    chemistry.

  - For Autistic people who have none of those issues, Cognitive
    Dysregulation may be what an old therapist of mine (he was 90 and
    had the thickest German accent you could imagine) called “stinkin’
    thinkin’.” It’s a thing. You can Google it.

  - As I mentioned earlier, anything other than a Positive Mental
    Attitude (see my section on Special Forces; this is an
    attitude/choice not an emotion) will lead you, inevitably, to a
    Cognitive Dysregulation. You, my dear Autistic reader, often travel
    in hostile territory. Keeping a Positive Mental Attitude is the
    difference between finding your way through the muck and getting
    stuck in it.

- Mimicry is an evolutionary response to environmental danger from
  predation. This, too, gets dysregulated and “almost” always does in
  Autistic people.

  - This is the core of the Autistic Destructive Mimicry Cycle discussed
    earlier.

  - This is reinforced by Fear, represented by bullies or shunners.

  - This is reinforced further by Grief, represented by parents, other
    loved ones or friends.

  - This is how we get from Masking to Meltdown to Shutdown to Burnout.

  - It’s also the way out, if we prune the Fear and Grief influences in
    our lives. Prune doesn’t mean eliminate; just reduce – drastically,
    as if your life depended upon it. I believe it does.

- Ever wonder why you’re not getting great sleep? Well, let’s see.

  - Your sensory system is angry because it’s overstimulated or
    under-stimulated or both – and in the words of every parent ever,
    you’re “over tired” (and therefore are not able to easily sleep).
    Perceptual dysregulation is beyond exhausting – it’s not tiring –
    it’s over-tiring. Sensory diet is the trick. More on that later.

  - If you’re like everyone else, you’re shining light from your phone,
    tablet or laptop right into your eyes within 2-3 hours of going to
    bed. You are literally telling your brain it’s time to wake up.
    Dark(er) is your friend, 2-3 hours before you go to bed.

  - If you didn’t have a meltdown today, congratulations! you had a
    great day. It still doesn’t mean you didn’t have RSD, PTSD or some
    other emotionally-triggering/upsetting thing that you’re now
    ruminating on. Emotional dysregulation isn’t exactly great bedtime
    preparation. Meditation and medication/supplements, however, might
    be. Work with your Autistic-affirming therapist or coach on this
    one.

  - You might have other issues going on where you’re sick, stomach
    hurts or have neurological issues. Medical attention is the call
    there. But we’ll get there.

- Immune Dysregulation; for most Autistic people this means you just
  “feel sick” all the time.

  - We talked about this in the context of the Autistic Parasite
    Response.

  - No, you likely don’t have a parasite (but if you think you do, of
    course go to the doctor).

  - People around you aren’t parasites. But I’m not sure your Autistic
    Immune System is convinced of that fact.

  - We talked about the symptoms of a “confused” immune system that
    behaves very similarly in three situations: parasitic infections,
    autoimmune diseases and Autism. “Oh!,” some might say...he just said
    Autism was a disease! No, I didn’t. I said the symptoms were similar
    to how the immune system responds under certain pressures, and
    that’s not the same thing.

  - This is Immune Dysregulation, my friends.

  - I believe that our environment (and I don’t mean heavy metals and
    preservatives in our strawberry jam) is making us sick. I think that
    too many bullies, shunners and gaslighters in our life are
    triggering an immune response.

  - Do an experiment. If you can, take a vacation (or medical leave)
    away from those types of people for 3-4 days – like really go away
    from them, no phone calls, nothing. Then let me about some of your
    Autistic “symptoms.” If you feel better, that’s diagnostic of
    something. If I’m right, your environment is triggering your Immune
    Dysregulation. Something, or several somethings, needs to change for
    you to live your best life.

- Gastrointestinal (GI) Dysregulation.

  - This goes together with the Immune Dysregulation like peas and
    carrots. One does not cause the other, necessarily. But a healthy
    Immune System is a major factor in having a healthy GI system. And
    it’s not one-direction only. Antibodies in the Immune System are
    made all over the body, including the GI system.

  - This could be IBS/IBS-D, constipation, diarrhea, gastroesophageal
    reflux or any number of GI issues.

  - We tend to favor samefood/safefoods that can prevent stomach upset.
    However, a lack of a balanced diet may be causing or contributing to
    GI issues. One of many, many issues that may be contributing to
    stomach issues. Anxiety, for one.

  - Dehydration, which often happens because Autistic people either
    forget to hydrate or don’t feel thirst through interoception,
    contributes to GI Issues. Stay hydrated (with hydration salts!).

  - Consider increasing (sloooowly) your fiber intake.

  - Consider pre or probiotics as part of your diet.

  - Limit caffeine, alcohol and spicy/fatty foods.

  - Eat smaller meals.

- Neurological Dysregulation.

  - There is a direct link to dysfunction in the GI Tract and any number
    of neurological issues (“dysregulations”).

  - Neurological Dysregulations are, without dancing around it,
    diseases. I am not saying Autism is a disease, but diseases travel
    with it. Epilepsy, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia/schizoaffective
    disorder and migraine are not to be played around with. You need a
    doctor treating you who is very good and specializes in your
    disease...so you can live your best life! Also, many neurological
    diseases <u>can be lethal if not treated</u>, so really there is no
    excuse or justification for not getting them treated. If you have
    one of these diseases, or *think you might*, and have someone around
    you saying don’t go to a specialist...do a life-saving thing and
    prune that person out of your life. Work with your Autistic
    therapist to help you find a great one.

  - Bipolar disorder and schizophrenia have direct ties back to GI
    Dysregulation/health. GI Dysregulation, as far as we know, is not
    the *only* cause of bipolar disorder and schizophrenia. But, word to
    the wise: ignoring your gut health is not going to help in any way,
    especially if you have bipolar disorder or schizophrenia.

  - And of course, Neurological Dysregulation impacts GI Dysregulation
    as well. I know when I’m “imbalanced” I go for the worst possible
    diet (“comfort food”).

  - It goes without saying that there is no Emotional Regulation without
    Neurological Regulation. Anyone who had a recent seizure or migraine
    will attest. These are Emotionally Dysregulating events, to say the
    least!

  - And, to come full circle, if you are having a migraine or seizure,
    your ability to have Perceptual Regulation is shot. Personally,
    while in the middle of a migraine, I can’t even see straight and all
    light and sound hurts, unbelievably.

- The image below is not perfect. It’s representative. This is not a
  perfect, in-order, representation of the Dysregulation that happens to
  every Autistic person on any given day.

- The core idea is that every dysregulation *dysregulates* another
  system, sometimes in multiple ways.

- The idea is that these are all part of a single mind/body system.
  There is an old joke, when the US sneezes, Latin America catches a
  cold. Not as true anymore, but I’d like to borrow it. If there is
  environmental discomfort that makes neurotypical people “sneeze” (a
  minor discomfort), Autistic people catch a cold. Small changes in one
  dysregulation make other types of dysregulations *much* more likely
  than for a neurotypical person. (Yet another reason to spend more time
  around Autistic people – they get it and get you.)

- But fear not! I had a manager that came to me once (at a company I
  used to work for) to evaluate his team for technical readiness. I
  found so many problems that, when I delivered my report, he was
  despondent: he didn’t know where to start. I told him, because there
  were so many problems, it didn’t really matter where he started
  because any improvement in any area would make all the others better!
  (Which was such an Autistic thing to say...I hope that helped him feel
  better, it would have me if positions were reversed...)

- This is the *upside* of having a system so easily and so often
  dysregulated. It’s also pretty easily kicked back into regulation (see
  what I did there? That's “Positive Mental Attitude”). You can pick any
  area you want – personally, I would start with Perceptual Regulation –
  I believe everything starts there, and without a balanced Perceptual
  System we simply can’t accurately know what’s happening in the world.
  We’re like a turtle stuck on its back. Luckily, through Sensory
  testing/tuning, we can mostly flip ourselves back over! (but don’t be
  surprised if you need help though adaptative devices, possible meds
  and likely some therapy)

- We do, I believe, need to focus on the Autistic world (community) that
  is designed for us, and stop trying to fit as much into the world that
  was not designed for us. Lean into our strengths as Autistic people,
  with Autistic people. When we stop trying to fit in with people who
  clearly aren’t making us feel welcome, life is bound to become better.
  As a 1970’s comedian (Harold Ramis) said/wrote famously, “Oh, I don’t
  have to put up with *this* abuse. I have dozens of people *lined up*
  to abuse me.”

- My Texan Grandmother, whenever I would invariably say “I have to go to
  bed” or “I have to do my homework” she would say, “You don’t have to
  do nothin’ but die and pay taxes. And if you die first, you don’t have
  to pay taxes.” We don’t have to spend most of our time with
  neurotypical people that bully, shun, gaslight and troll us. We may
  *need to* spend some time around them in the short-term for job income
  or housing support. But long term, there are alternatives awaiting us
  in the Autistic Community. But to get there, we need a Positive Mental
  Attitude and a willingness to set “pruning them” as a goal.

- The days of Autistic people trying to fit into the neurotypical world
  need to come, if not to a screeching halt, at least become a *highly
  restricted diet*.
