## Chapter 13: NT Shame Cycle: The Engine of NT Destructiveness towards Autistic People

<img src="media/image2.png" style="width:4.74498in;height:8in" />

We often think of rage as showing up in the classroom or workplace
bullying, and this is part of it.   This is the top cycle that
accelerates the secondary cycle for the Autistic person: “the beatings
will continue until morale \[or behavior\] improves.” But this abuse
extends into psychological techniques, microaggressions from teachers,
parents, and "friends” or peers. Medically, ABA and even CBT, further
the microaggressions that are a form of micro-institutionalization. All
of this traumatizes the Autistic person. 

The NT Shame Cycle has a physiological impact on Autistic people, I
believe in part due to hypersensitivity to sensory input, driving an
acute stress response.

Autistic Acute Stress Response to NT Shame Cycle

- Camouflaging/Masking<sup>187</sup> (flight)

- Avoiding/Compensating<sup>187</sup> (flight)

- Acquiescing/Assimilating<sup>187</sup> (fawn/feign)

- Meltdown (fight)

- Shutdown (freeze)

- Burnout (freeze)

This is a positive feedback loop for Autistic people, although it is not
“positive” at all, in the sense of beneficial to the Autistic person.
It’s driving predictably destructive outcomes. Higher stress. Lower
self-worth. Higher incidence of illness, both short term and long-term.
Over time, if this stress continues (and it almost always does), shorter
lifespan through toxic-stress related illnesses.

As we are social animals, the drive within us to connect with each other
is almost paramount. But for Autistic people the *only option presented*
of socially acceptable behavior becomes a deep denial of one’s own
values and needs. Since needs are not met and in fact are taught over a
lifetime that Autistic needs & values do not matter, only behavior that
complies with societally sanctioned inauthentic camouflaging matters.
This message is drilled so hard into the minds of Autistic people that
at times all intellectual resources are diverted to maintaining
appearances. Through suppressed interoception, which may be exacerbated
by prioritizing masking at all costs, unmet needs build within a
hydraulic model of hyperarousal of the sympathetic nervous system. With
hydraulic models, especially one centered on hyperarousal, the pressure
builds over a period of months and years within the Autistic Acute
Stress Response to NT Shame Cycle.

Then there is the issue of agency. When one camouflages for social
status or other personal gain, as we see with NT people, the person will
still feel the effects of unmet needs and betrayed values. However,
camouflaging/masking out of a sense of survival, e.g., to prevent abuse,
is beyond a feeling – it becomes layered trauma, known as Complex PTSD
(C-PTSD). The NT version of camouflaging is social climbing and often
feels like a choice, even if there is no other alternative. The Autistic
case can feel like a never-ending cycle of trauma from which one cannot
possibly escape. We’ll get back to this last point later in the book,
and more importantly how to stop it, reverse it and heal.

These behaviors of masking and the rest don’t happen in a vacuum.
Something is driving behaviors rooted deep in our biology through
evolutionary strategies for survival. We’ll start to talk about this
now, but then pick this up later in the book when we talk about
Monotropism vs. Intense World Theories.
