I don’t understand substance abuse apart from toxic stress. You have been through a lot to get to this point. To be addicted or to have a substance abuse issue means that there are untreated wounds/pain, likely trauma, and severe long-term unmet needs in your life. You owe it to yourself to seek treatment, stick with it, and look at the people, environments and commitments you are making that made substance abuse more likely: and prune them. This is not a moral weakness or a failing on your part – but given how powerful the drugs are now you will need help to recover.

There is also a silent epidemic amongst Autistic people which is sexual abuse – and this is a major contributor to substance abuse and addiction. It is very important that Autistic people, starting in pre-adolescence, get the facts regarding sexual health and relationship boundaries. A great resource for learning the boundaries of healthy relationships is the series of podcasts here: https://researchautism.org/self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates-podcast/ And, it is very important that Autistic people have a healthy relationship with sex, which starts with regular self-pleasure or masturbation. Orgasms release oxytocin, which in addition to counteracting cortisol (a stress hormone), can also counteract some of the effects of toxic stress, also helps us connect with our bodies in a way that promotes somatic mindfulness208. People who have both Autism and suffer with addiction may carry extra stress, so that natural release is perhaps even more important than for other Autistic people. There is also some evidence that regular orgasms may help alleviate the symptoms of some forms of substance abuse/addiction207. This was discussed during the Stimming Chapter, and because the intersection of sex and Autism carries such a strong taboo it’s critical that Autistic people connect early with their bodies in a healthy, pleasurable way.
