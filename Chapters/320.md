In this chapter, I am not distinguishing between any variation in the LGBTQIA+ spectrum. If you consider yourself a woman, this section is for you. Are there differences between gender-at-birth and not when it comes to the struggles each encounter, depending upon environment? Of course, there are. But in general women experience misogyny through a global patriarchal power structure that exists solely to oppress the expression of female power/ability in all its manifestations.

Women, as a rule, are more likely to mask and at an earlier age. This much you know. Women, as Autistic people, are also much more likely to deeply mask, which means compensation and assimilation. Since I’m a heavy assimilator; I know firsthand that this is not good for anyone’s health, especially long-term. This is a problem, because Women are less likely to get diagnosed (early) and since symptoms are vastly unrecognized and underreported as a result, they don’t get the support and services they need to build and maintain a healthy life. They are also much more likely to be bullied, shunned and gaslit as a result. Tragically, and consequently, they are much more likely to experience assault and sexual abuse than their NT and Male Autistic counterparts. Diagnosis is possible and many have taken advantage of free online resources such as https://embrace-autism.com/autism-tests/ specifically Cat-Q (for masking/camouflaging) and RAADS-Revised.

And, not surprisingly after knowing all of this, Women are up to four times more likely to die from MD than Autistic males. The Autistic Male MD statistics are 4 times higher relative to the general population. But the odd thing is that in the NT community, men are many times more likely to die from MD than women. Not so for Autistic Women. If Autistic MD is a pandemic in men at 4 times their NT counterparts, then I don’t know what to call Autistic MD amongst Women at 16 times their NT counterparts other than a silent pandemic and an outrage that shocks the conscience.

There is also a silent epidemic amongst Autistic women which is sexual abuse. It is very important that Autistic women, starting in pre-adolescence, get the facts regarding sexual health and relationship boundaries. A great resource for learning the boundaries of healthy relationships is the series of podcasts here: https://researchautism.org/self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates-podcast/ And, it is very important that Autistic women have a healthy relationship with sex, which starts with self-pleasure or masturbation with or without orgasm. If possible, orgasm is a healthy addition as it releases oxytocin, which in addition to counteracting cortisol (a stress hormone), can also counteract some of the effects of toxic stress, also helps connect women with their own bodies in a natural way. This was discussed during the Stimming Chapter, and because the intersection of sex, women and Autism carries such a strong taboo it’s critical that women connect in a healthy way early with their bodies in a healthy, pleasurable way.

You are disabled, and most of this book is dedicated to helping make sure your disability doesn’t make you ill or worse. In fact, many of the steps in the book will move you towards wellness. I believe many of the steps herein will save your life. But make no mistake, I don’t not conflate my white male experience with yours. I walk through the world, and if I mask (and really, don’t ever talk), I am treated with privilege. Women’s rights are a political struggle and will continue to be so for all women and I hope a growing number of men. There is no way to separate the intersectionality of you as a Woman from you as an Autistic person. Both are dangerous at times. But I believe in the ideals I learned at Toyota; the so-called “prioritized problem at the point of occurrence.” I believe most of you would see Autism as the priority, and certainly most of you who are reading this book. Focus on the migraine first, then the other health concerns – for no one can focus on anything else when your head is coming apart. Autism is the proverbial migraine; settle any dysregulations (Emotional, Sensory, etc) related to Autism first and then you will be stronger to take on the evils of misogyny at work in your life. You will also be better positioned to enlist the help of others of all genders and intersex identities in fighting for the rights of Women everywhere.
