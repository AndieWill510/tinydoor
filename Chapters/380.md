For some Bipolar (I or II) is a neurotype. For others, it’s a disorder. I’m not going to wade into that argument, because regardless of your point of view, Bipolar can be a major contributing factor to emotional dysregulation in an Autistic person’s life. As the old saying goes, “it’s not a problem until it is, and when it is [a problem], it really is.”

The general rule is that if you believe that you have bipolar disorder, you need to be under the care of a great psychiatrist. How do you know? You’ve had a psychotic episode (or hypomania with psychotic features) and likely hospitalized for it. I’ve said elsewhere that there are great psychiatrists and there are bad ones. You need referrals (preferably several) from people you know and/or doctors you trust to get to a great one. Fire the bad ones fast: bipolar is a grave mental illness and you need a great doctor treating you. Also, get stable as fast as you can and stay compliant with your medication. Get regular sleep and regular exercise. This is all part of how you will stay well. You are worth it. Your life may depend upon you staying on your meds.

There is also a silent epidemic amongst Autistic people which is sexual abuse. It is very important that Autistic people, starting in pre-adolescence, get the facts regarding sexual health and relationship boundaries. A great resource for learning the boundaries of healthy relationships is the series of podcasts here: https://researchautism.org/self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates/sex-ed-for-self-advocates-podcast/ And, it is very important that Autistic people have a healthy relationship with sex, which starts with regular self-pleasure or masturbation. Orgasms release oxytocin, which in addition to counteracting cortisol (a stress hormone), can also counteract some of the effects of toxic stress, also helps us connect with our bodies in a way that promotes somatic mindfulness208. People who have both Autism and Bipolar may carry extra stress, so that natural release is perhaps even more important than for other Autistic people. This was discussed during the Stimming Chapter, and because the intersection of sex and Autism carries such a strong taboo it’s critical that Autistic people connect early with their bodies in a healthy, pleasurable way.
