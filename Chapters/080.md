How Does ABA Harm Autistic People (<span class="mark">trigger
warning</span>)

ABA techniques are defined as: stimuli or events that are unpleasant or
painful for the individual, and that are used to decrease or eliminate
unwanted behaviors. ABA techniques can be

1.  physical abuse, such as

- electric shocks (modified cattle prods)

- sprays

- pinches

- slaps

- other physical assault

2.  psychological abuse, such as

- verbal reprimands

- social isolation (solitary confinement)

- humiliation

All of this takes place in a closed (and sometimes locked) room with a
grown adult and a disabled child. ABA techniques are based on the
principle of negative reinforcement or punishment, which means that the
removal or application of an aversive stimulus increases or decreases
the likelihood of a behavior occurring in the future. ABA argues it only
now uses positive reinforcement. But, negative or positive, it doesn’t
matter because *withholding* a positive or applying an aversive has the
same net impact, *or they wouldn’t do either one.* The goal is to alter
behavior through manipulation, coercion and implied or overt threats to
a disabled child: i.e., abuse. A recent study showed that:

> “...460 respondents (autistic adults and caregivers of autistic
> children), found those exposed to ABA were 86 per cent more likely to
> meet the criteria for post-traumatic stress disorder than those not
> exposed to ABA”

We can call it “operant conditioning,” but that’s a euphemism. Power
over someone vulnerable, someone disabled where the impact of
manipulation is magnified many times over (due to sensory sensitivity or
in some cases desensitivity) compared to a non-Autistic person would
feel, *is* the point. Whether the Autistic child is aware of what is
happening not, the body registers this treatment as trauma and PTSD is
the result.

ABA techniques (rewards and aversives) have been used in various
settings and contexts, such as animal training, addiction treatment,
behavior modification, and "conversion therapy." Many people, including
governments, who have studied this in depth now consider “conversion
therapy,” of which Autism and Gay children fit, to be a form of torture.
When you have torture, you get trauma and if not PTSD then C-PTSD. Cause
and effect.
