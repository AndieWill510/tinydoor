Our world is too bright. It’s too loud. It smells bad. Clothes are too scratchy. And there are entirely too many people making entirely too many demands. It doesn’t have to be that way. We must stand up for what we need in the simplest of ways:

Less light

Less noise

Less smells

Less scratchy

People are often unkind to us. They may or may not intend to, but they are. They bully, shun, assault and worst of all gaslight us to believe that “none of this could possibly be as bad as we’re making it out to be.” Again, we must stand up for what we need by spending less time with:

Bullies

Shunners

Abusers

Gaslighters

We must find ways to stim more to alleviate stress. Fidget toys are helpful for autistic people to manage anxiety, self-regulate, and calm down. There are many types of fidget toys that can suit different sensory preferences and needs. Some of the best fidget toys for autism are12345:

- Dodecagon: This is a 12-sided fidget toy that has different functions on each side, such as buttons, switches, gears, joysticks, and more. It can provide tactile, auditory, and visual stimulation for people who like to fidget with their hands¹.

- Reversible Sequin Hand Fidget: This is a soft and flexible toy that has sequins on both sides that can be flipped to reveal different colors. It can provide tactile and visual stimulation for people who like to play with textures and patterns¹.

- Infinity Cube: This is a metal cube that can be folded and unfolded endlessly. It can provide tactile and visual stimulation for people who like to manipulate objects and watch them change shapes4.

- Pop It: This is a silicone toy that has bubbles that can be popped and pushed. It can provide tactile, auditory, and visual stimulation for people who like to pop bubble wrap or press buttons³.

- Chewelry: This is a term for jewelry that can be chewed safely. It can provide oral stimulation for people who like to bite or suck on things.

- Spinning toys: These are toys that can spin or rotate, such as fidget spinners, tops, or gyroscopes. They can provide visual and vestibular stimulation for people who like to watch or feel spinning motion³5.

- Squishy Stress Relief Balls: These are balls that can be squeezed, bounced, or thrown. They can provide tactile and proprioceptive stimulation for people who like to apply pressure or release tension¹.

We have a healthcare system designed to misunderstand us, misdiagnose us, mistreat us and finally adding insult to injury rush us through conversations that simply cannot be rushed. Again, we must stand up for what we need by engaging with a team that can provide:

Autistic medical coordinators

Autistic medical advocates

Autistic coaches

Autistic-affirming therapists and doctors

We have an employment system that was designed to exclude us, denigrate us, terminate us and ensure we stay unemployable. Again, we must stand up for what we need by engaging with a team that can provide:

Interview and onboarding coaches

Reasonable accommodation brokers

Career coaches

Conflict resolution specialists

One of the aspects of Autism is that we are a constellation of people, and the “intersectionalities” of the communities we belong to are as varied as we are. Am I a white male? Yeah, if you believe my DNA tests, I’m more British than the Royal Family. So, there’s that. I walk around with all that privilege. But I’m also Bipolar I and struggle with Psychosis and Social Anxiety. I have ADHD or am AuDHD. I have asthma and am deathly allergic to cats and nicotine/smoking in general. I’m a survivor of industrial grade Adverse Childhood Events “ACE” (prolonged abuse by primary caretakers inside my nuclear family). My Sensory Profile is about as avoidant as you could imagine; everything in the world seems to dysregulate me. I’m not one thing: thus the concept of “intersectionalities.” I am all these things, and they make up who I am, but also how things “lands” makes me different from many NT people. Makes me different from you as well, even though we’re both Autistic. But that doesn’t mean we can’t have a meaningful conversation about the impacts upon these different intersectionalities. It just means that I’m not an expert on what I’m not experienced in.
