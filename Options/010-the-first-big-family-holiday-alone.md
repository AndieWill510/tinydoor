# Option 1.10 — The First Big Family Holiday Alone

## Doorway

Memorial Day 2024 was the first big family holiday after the Christmas holiday season that almost ended me.

I was alone.

That is not a poetic way to say I was sad.

It is a practical fact.

I needed something to do.

Sometimes survival begins with needing the day not to eat you alive.

## Pressure

Holidays can make absence loud.

They can turn ordinary loneliness into architecture.

Everyone else seems to have a table, a plan, a group text.

And you have time.

Too much time.

Time with teeth.

I did not need a perfect plan.

I needed somewhere to put my body while the day passed through me.

There were two UNESCO World Heritage Sites two hours south.

In one town.

A town I knew only in the worst way.

And that never stopped an Autistic person.

## Reframe

We know what it is to be blamed for something that has nothing to do with us.

We know what it is to walk toward a room that has already decided something about us.

So I went.

Not because I was brave.

Not because I was healed.

I went because the day needed somewhere to put me.

This is one version of another option.

Not a grand solution.

A motion.

A road.

A place to go before the day could close around me.

## Move

When the day is too large, ask:

> Where can I safely put my body for the next hour?

One hour.

One place.

Not forever.

Let small motion count.

## Mercy

A distraction can still be mercy.

And sometimes the thing you do because you cannot bear the day becomes the first road toward home.
