# Option 5.9
# Fewer Fires, Not More Grit

## A Way In

When everything is on fire, people may praise grit. Keep going. Push through. Be resilient. Hold it together.

But a body in Autistic burnout does not need a speech about fireproofing. It needs less radiation exposure.

## Pressure

Autistic burnout can be treated as a personal stamina problem: not enough discipline, attitude, productivity tools, or positive thinking. So the person tries harder inside the same conditions that are burning them down.

More masking. More compensation. More apology. More recovery stolen from sleep. More pretending the body has not already sent the warning.

That is not recovery. That is radiation exposure with better manners.

## Reframe

Burnout is not proof that you failed to become strong enough. It is information about load, threat, mismatch, unmet need, and recovery debt — the cost the body carries when rest keeps getting postponed.

Support may include therapy, values work, body-based care, medical care, accommodation, community, food, sleep, money, housing, and fewer punishing rooms. No single door has to be the whole cure.

The tiny door is the first reduction in fire. Not a perfect life. Less flame. More soothing water. One place where the body is not required to keep proving it can survive what is hurting it.

## Move

Pick one fire you can reduce by one notch: a meeting, a social obligation, a sensory demand, a deadline, or a conversation that always costs too much. Ask:

> What would make this one notch less burning?

Maybe shorter, quieter, written, later, with food first, with permission to leave, or with no explanation until after recovery. Choose one reduction.

## Mercy

You do not have to heal inside the same fire that burned you. Recovery is not more grit. Recovery starts when the body gets less radiation exposure.
