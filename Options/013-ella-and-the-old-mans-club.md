# Option 1.13
# Ella and the Old Man's Club

## A Way In

Then walked in Ella.

Larger than her own life, much less everyone else's.

A flapper with a very punk rock edge.

She gave no shits about anyone's attitude.

And frankly, she was exactly what I needed.

Some people enter a room like permission.

Ella in entered like the room had already lost the argument.

## Pressure

When you have spent a life trying to earn your way into rooms, a person who refuses the room's judgment can feel almost impossible.

Not safe in the soft way.

Safe in the disruptive way.

Safe because she did not seem to organize herself around whether other people approved.

Sometimes belonging kicks the door open and says:

> Come on. We're going somewhere better.

## Reframe

Then came the question.

Did I want to come to an art opening?

Yes.

It was Coach's.

Oh hell yes.

That decision, and meeting Dave, would prove fateful in the most delicious way.

Dave invited me to join the "old man's club."

First for a single meeting.

Then later, much later, as a member.

Dave did not invite anyone to join.

Sometimes the beam that carries the house is an invitation someone almost never gives.

## Move

Think of one invitation that changed more than it seemed to change.

One door.

One yes.

Do not make it smaller because you did not know yet what it would become.

## Mercy

Belonging does not always arrive as a plan.

Sometimes it arrives through a door nobody was supposed to open for you.
