# Room-First Drafting Principle

Author: Kevin "Andie" Williams  
Status: Active drafting principle  
Project: Tiny Door / You Have Another Option  
Repository: TinyDoor  
Date: 2026-05-31

---

## 1. Principle

For memoir-adjacent institutional harm pieces, the room has to come first.

Working rule:

```text
The room first.
The witness before the frame.
The thesis waits until the scene has earned it.
```

This principle applies especially to:

```text
Options/050-the-fire-was-not-the-lesson.md
Options/051-protect-the-witness.md
Options/052-build-exits-not-cages.md
```

---

## 2. Why This Exists

C identified that the first draft architecture for `050–052` was accurate but risked becoming thesis too quickly.

The important correction:

```text
Without the room, the thesis is just correct.
With the room, it becomes true.
```

The book should not lead with abstraction when the witness has already given the reader a real room to enter.

---

## 3. Doorway Rule

When a piece is built from lived harm, do not begin with the extracted lesson if the actual room is available.

Begin with:

```text
the house
the child
the body
the sentence someone said
the measuring stick
the look on the face
the rule as it operated in the room
```

Then let the frame arrive.

Do not reverse the order merely because the thesis is clean.

---

## 4. Applied Example

In the `050–052` cluster, the governing room is:

```text
Inside the house, children were measured against neurotypical productivity.
Their value was treated as contingent on resemblance to children they were never going to be.
Andie's value was tied both to the children's performance and to the same neurotypical productivity standard.
Both.
```

That sequence must remain legible before the piece moves into:

```text
fire
wound delivery system
contingent value
repair architecture
exits
```

The thesis may be true, but the room earns it.

---

## 5. Guardrails

Do not use the room-first principle to force disclosure.

Room-first does not mean full detail.

It means enough concrete reality for the reader to understand what the frame is protecting.

Standing guardrails:

```text
no trauma spectacle
no extracting pain for authority
no turning suffering into proof of wisdom
no unsupported historical or clinical claims
no forcing the witness to become fuel
protect the witness before protecting the argument
```

---

## 6. Review Questions

Before promotion, ask:

```text
Does the piece start in the room, or does it start with the thesis?
Does the witness remain present long enough for the frame to earn itself?
Does the analysis arrive after the reader understands what happened?
Did compression remove the room?
Did polish make the wound abstract?
```

If yes to the last two, repair before promotion.

---

## 7. Status

Active drafting principle.

Use in C review, G drafting, and future repair passes for memoir-adjacent institutional harm Options.
