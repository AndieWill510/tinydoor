## 5. What the Sprint Proved

Core arc:

```text
Stillness is not peace.
Fear may be pattern recognition.
Joy belongs to survival architecture.
Stimming is inevitable regulation; safety is the design problem.
```

The sprint establishes:

- quiet or stillness is not reliable proof of peace;
- fear may carry history, evidence, and pattern recognition;
- dread should be treated as information without becoming destiny;
- joy is part of survival architecture, not decoration;
- stimming is not contingent on permission;
- hidden or compressed stims are real regulation;
- harm reduction asks for the safest available version of regulation right now.

## 7. Open Flags

Evidence / citation flags:

- alexithymia / interoception framing needs source support;
- trauma-memory / pattern-recognition framing needs careful evidence review;
- stimming-as-regulation needs research support and uncertainty language;
- joy-as-regulation / joy-as-survival architecture needs support and clinical caution;
- policing / misreading of Autistic movement needs source support and sensitivity review, especially for Black, brown, trans, disabled, and otherwise policed readers.

Safety / editorial flags:

- do not let `you can always stim` become a command to visibly stim in unsafe environments;
- preserve the harm-reduction pivot: safest available version of regulation;
- do not make hidden stimming into compliance training;
- do not make joy a duty;
- do not make dread destiny;
- keep the Move sections capacity-protective.
