# Book Architecture — Turning the Imported Manuscript into a Survival Interface

Author: Kevin "Andie" Williams  
Status: Draft v0.1  
Project: You Have Another Option  
Repository: AnotherOption  
Date: 2026-05-30

---

## 1. Controlling Principle

The imported files are source material, not destiny.

The current 44 chapter files are not the final reader-facing structure.

They are imported manuscript chunks.

They preserve source material, sequence, and provenance. They do not determine the final book shape.

The final book should be structured around reader survival, not manuscript inheritance.

---

## 2. Reader Interface Principle

The book should become a survival interface.

A reader should be able to enter the book while overwhelmed, ashamed, exhausted, overloaded, frightened, hiding, recovering, or trying to keep going.

The unit of reading should be small enough to hold under stress.

Working rule:

> One private sitting. One survivable idea. One concrete next move.

A chapter is not a warehouse.

A chapter is a regulated breath.

---

## 3. Architecture Decision

Do not edit the 44 imported files one by one as if they are final chapters.

Instead:

1. Treat the 44 files as source bins.
2. Collapse them into a smaller reader-facing book architecture.
3. Extract short "Options" from each source bin.
4. Preserve larger source chunks for provenance.
5. Edit for continuity only after the architecture is stable.

This avoids premature fine-grained editing.

---

## 4. Target Reader-Facing Shape

Working target:

- 7 parts;
- approximately 12 reader-facing chapters;
- 60–120 short Options;
- each Option readable in one private pause;
- each Option carries one idea, one mercy, and one next move.

The imported chapter count may remain 44 in `Chapters/`.

The reader-facing chapter count should be much smaller.

## 7. Editing Passes

### Pass 1 — Architecture

Goal: decide where each source bin belongs.

Do not fine-edit prose.

Do not solve continuity.

Do not chase citations except to mark high-risk claims.

Output:

- stable part structure;
- reader-facing chapter list;
- source-to-destination map;
- extraction queue for Options.

### Pass 2 — Option Extraction

Goal: extract short, bathroom-readable units.

Each Option should carry:

- one pressure;
- one reframe;
- one practical move;
- one closing mercy or mantra.

Output:

- 60–120 Options;
- each Option mapped to source bins;
- each Option given a provisional title.

### Pass 3 — Reader Safety Review

Goal: check whether distressed readers are made safer.

Look for:

- method detail risk;
- fatalism;
- shame leakage;
- overpromising;
- “simple fixes” language;
- advice that assumes resources the reader may not have;
- rhetoric that becomes heavier than the relief it offers.

### Pass 4 — C Challenge Review

Goal: ask C to challenge the scaffold, not the voice.

C should test:

- whether the structure overreaches;
- whether scientific claims need more humility;
- whether the reader path gives relief early enough;
- whether Part VI avoids tokenization;
- whether Autism, Inc. material overwhelms the survival path;
- whether the architecture preserves joy as survival infrastructure.

### Pass 5 — Voice and Continuity

Goal: edit the book after the structure exists.

Only now should the prose be smoothed chapter by chapter.

## 8. Option Naming Convention

Use `Option` as the small unit name.

Rationale:

- the title is *You Have Another Option*;
- the reader should repeatedly experience choice returning;
- “Option” is practical, gentle, non-clinical, and non-heroic;
- each Option can be read in one private pause.

Format:

```text
Option <chapter>.<number> — <Title>
```

Examples:

```text
Option 9.2 — Learn to Swim Before You Need the Water
Option 9.4 — Epilepsy Needs a Neurologist
Option 9.5 — Call 988 Before You Negotiate
Option 10.3 — Have a Designated Exit Person
Option 11.9 — Addiction Is Often Survival That Became Dangerous
```

## 9. High-Risk Architecture Issues

These must be challenged before final structure:

1. **Mortality claims** — require careful citation and methodological humility.
2. **MD terminology** — may need replacement or reframing before reader-facing publication.
3. **ABA critique** — should remain fierce, but rigor must be protected.
4. **Sex-positive Autism** — needs scope, audience, age-safety, and trauma-safety review.
5. **Part VI** — must not become a tokenized special-populations appendix.
6. **Autism, Inc.** — important, but may need compression so the book remains survival-forward.
7. **References** — should probably become back matter, not a chapter.
8. **Long trauma/cortisol paper material** — should probably become evidence support, not a reader-facing chapter.
