## 4. Claim Cluster A — Behaviorism / ABA Uses Observable Behavior as Primary Target

### Draft claim

ABA / behavior analysis traditionally focuses on observable behavior and behavior-environment relations.

### Evidence found

Search results and public summaries identify ABA as an applied science using respondent and operant conditioning to change behavior, and distinguish it from experimental behavior analysis and radical behaviorism.

The public summary also notes that ABA replaced the older language of behavior modification because behavior modification implied changing behavior without clarifying behavior-environment relations.

### Evidence register

```text
historical / disciplinary definition
```

### Source candidates

- Cooper, Heron, Heward, *Applied Behavior Analysis*.
- Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis / field definitions.
- Wikipedia summary as locator only: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_behavior_analysis

### Reader-facing caution

Do not overstate this as "ABA ignores mental health" without source support and nuance.

Safer language:

```text
ABA has historically centered observable behavior and behavior-environment relations. That emphasis can become dangerous when the person’s internal experience, consent, distress, or cost disappears from the analysis.
```

## 5. Claim Cluster B — Lovaas's Early Autism Work Used Aversives

### Draft claim

Lovaas's early work with autistic children included aversive methods, including physical punishment / electric shock, although the use and acceptability of aversives changed over time.

### Evidence found

Public summaries consistently identify Lovaas as a pioneer of ABA / discrete trial training for autistic children and note that his early methods included aversives such as slaps and electric shocks.

A likely source cited in multiple indexes:

```text
Bowman, Rachel A. "Screams, Slaps, and Love: The Strange Birth of Applied Behavior Analysis." Pediatrics. 2014.
```

Other source candidates:

```text
"Building social behavior in autistic children by use of electric shock." Journal of Experimental Research in Personality. 1965.
"After you hit a child, you can't just get up and leave him; you are hooked to that kid." Psychology Today interview with Ole Ivar Lovaas, 1974.
```

### Evidence register

```text
historical claim / requires primary-source verification
```

### Reader-facing caution

Do not imply all current ABA uses these methods.

Safer language:

```text
Some foundational ABA/autism work included aversives that would now be ethically unacceptable in many contexts. Current practices vary, and the evidence pass must distinguish historical practice, current practice, and reform claims.
```

## 6. Claim Cluster C — Rekers / Lovaas 1974 JABA Paper Used Behavioral Methods to Change Gender Nonconforming Behavior

### Draft claim

In 1974, George Rekers and O. Ivar Lovaas published "Behavioral Treatment of Deviant Sex-Role Behaviors in a Male Child" in the *Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis*.

The paper is widely discussed as a behavioral intervention targeting gender nonconforming behavior in a young child and as part of the history of conversion therapy.

### Evidence found

Public search results repeatedly identify the paper:

```text
George A. Rekers, O. Ivar Lovaas. "Behavioral Treatment of Deviant Sex-Role Behaviors in a Male Child." Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis. 1974.
```

Wiley/JABA access appears to block direct fetch in browser, but the title and publication details are consistently indexed.

Likely original DOI / locator candidate:

```text
https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1901/jaba.1974.7-173
```

### Evidence register

```text
primary source identified / direct access still needed
```

### Reader-facing caution

The book can safely state that the paper exists and that it targeted gender nonconforming behavior.

Avoid claiming causal responsibility for later death without careful attribution and source support.

Safer language:

```text
The same behavioral tradition also appears in published work aimed at changing a child’s gender nonconforming behavior. That history matters, even before we argue what it means.
```

## 7. Claim Cluster D — JABA Issued an Expression of Concern / Editor's Note Rather Than a Retraction

### Draft claim

In 2020, JABA issued an expression of concern and editor's note about the 1974 Rekers / Lovaas paper, acknowledging harm and stating why the journal did not retract the paper.

### Evidence found

Search results consistently identify:

```text
Expression of Concern. Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis. 2020.
Editor's Note: Societal changes and expression of concern about Rekers and Lovaas' (1974) Behavioral Treatment of Deviant Sex-Role Behaviors in a Male Child. Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis. 2020.
```

Public summaries report that JABA said the work would violate current ethical standards but did not retract it because of the journal's view of standards at the time.

### Evidence register

```text
primary source identified / direct access still needed
```

### Source candidates

- Wiley/JABA expression of concern and editor's note.
- Retraction Watch coverage: "Journal flags — but does not retract — decades-old paper on 'correcting' gender identity".

### Reader-facing caution

Do not say JABA retracted the paper unless primary evidence confirms retraction. Current recovered evidence says expression of concern / editor's note, not retraction.

Safer language:

```text
The journal later flagged the paper with an expression of concern, not a retraction.
```

## 8. Claim Cluster E — Conversion Therapy / ABA Overlap Is Real but Must Be Stated Carefully

### Draft claim

There is documented overlap between ABA methods and historical conversion-therapy practices, especially through Rekers / Lovaas and later scholarship analyzing the queer history of autism science.

### Evidence found

Source candidates from repo references and search trail:

```text
Conine, Campau, Petronelli. "LGBTQ+ conversion therapy and applied behavior analysis: A call to action." Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis. 2022.
Gibson, Margaret F.; Douglas, Patty. "Disturbing Behaviours: Ole Ivar Lovaas and the Queer History of Autism Science." Catalyst: Feminism, Theory, Technoscience. 2018.
Human Rights Campaign: "The Lies and Dangers of Reparative Therapy." https://www.hrc.org/resources/the-lies-and-dangers-of-reparative-therapy
Born Perfect conversion therapy bans: https://bornperfect.org/facts/conversion-therapy-bans-by-state/
ABAI Policy Statement on Conversion Therapy and Practices.
```

### Evidence register

```text
historical / scholarly interpretation / policy claim
```

### Reader-facing caution

Do not flatten this into "ABA equals conversion therapy" without attribution and nuance.

Safer language:

```text
Some ABA history directly overlaps with conversion-therapy history. That does not prove every present-day ABA practice is identical to conversion therapy, but it does mean the normalization goal must be examined with extraordinary care.
```

Autism intervention outcomes should not treat "passing" or indistinguishability from non-autistic peers as the main sign of success.

```text
Ne'eman, Ari; Richman, Kenneth A.; McCarthy, Allison M.; Wilkenfeld, Daniel. "A Passing Problem: Evaluating Harm and Benefit in Autism Research." Ethics & Human Research. 2023.
```

### Evidence register

```text
ethics / disability studies / outcome-measure critique
```

### Reader-facing caution

Use this as support for Options 041–042, not as a substitute for explaining the argument.

Safer language:

```text
If the measured success is that the person looks less autistic, the measure itself must be challenged.
```

## 10. Claim Cluster G — Current ABA Evidence / Harm Claims Are Contested and Need Separation

### Draft claim

There is evidence that some behavioral interventions can improve certain skills or outcomes for some autistic children, and there are also significant ethical, methodological, and lived-experience critiques of ABA and ABA-derived therapies.

### Evidence found

Relevant source candidates from search trail:

```text
Rachel K. Schuck et al. "Neurodiversity and Autism Intervention: Reconciling Perspectives Through a Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Intervention Framework." Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders. 2022.
Geraldine Dawson et al. "At a Crossroads—Reconsidering the Goals of Autism Early Behavioral Intervention From a Neurodiversity Perspective." JAMA Pediatrics. 2022.
Owen McGill, Anna Robinson. "Recalling hidden harms": autistic experiences of childhood applied behavioural analysis (ABA). Advances in Autism. 2021.
Laura K. Anderson. "Autistic experiences of applied behavior analysis." Autism. 2023.
```

### Evidence register

```text
mixed evidence / contested practice / lived-experience critique
```

### Reader-facing caution

Do not write as if the evidence question is simple.

Separate:

```text
skills gained
harm experienced
current practice variation
historical practice
normalization goals
research quality
lived-experience testimony
```
