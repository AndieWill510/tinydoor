## 6. Specific Review Questions

Please answer directly:

1. Does 028 extend 027 cleanly, or does it repeat 027 too much?
2. Does 028 preserve harm-reduction without making hostile rooms seem acceptable?
3. Does 028's river / channel metaphor work, or does it over-poeticize safety?
4. Does 029 handle masking with enough nuance: strategy, armor, cost, and not a moral failure?
5. Does 029 risk making masking sound necessary or admirable rather than sometimes protective and costly?
6. Does 029's phrase “compressed regulation” work as candidate house language?
7. Does 030 validate meltdown / shutdown / overload without excusing harm or removing accountability?
8. Does 030's “body chooses” framing land safely, or does it risk over-agency / under-agency confusion?
9. Do the Move sections remain small enough for low-capacity readers?
10. What is the smallest safe repair package?

---

## 7. Safety Questions

Please pay special attention to:

- hostile environments;
- visible vs hidden stimming;
- masking as survival vs masking as demand;
- burnout / shutdown / meltdown language;
- accountability and repair after harm without reader-blaming;
- not implying the body is always safe or always correct;
- not implying the reader is responsible for unsafe rooms;
- not turning harm reduction into compliance training;
- whether each Move offers a tiny door.

---

## 8. Evidence / Overclaim Questions

Please flag where later evidence or clinical review is needed:

- masking and burnout;
- stimming and regulation;
- shutdown / meltdown / overload;
- alexithymia / interoception if implicated;
- trauma and nervous-system response;
- harm-reduction framing in disability / Autistic contexts.

Do not require citations at this drafting stage unless a claim is dangerously over-specific.
