## 4. What the Sprint Proved

Core arc:

```text
Recovery before analysis.
Autistic first aid before diplomacy.
After-action reports are maps, not confessions.
```

The sprint establishes:

- immediate post-overload time should not become a courtroom;
- a flooded nervous system may need recovery before explanation;
- Autistic first aid should be reachable, ordinary, and practical;
- scripts, food, water, dark, quiet, pressure, sleep, and safe stimming can be part of recovery planning;
- later analysis should look for load, missing supports, and one notch of change;
- reflection should not become self-prosecution.

## 6. Open Flags

Evidence / clinical / editorial flags:

- clarify that Autistic first aid is not medical advice;
- ensure recovery suggestions remain flexible and accessible to readers with different bodies, housing, jobs, families, disabilities, and support levels;
- avoid implying repair can always wait when immediate safety or relational harm requires urgent action;
- ensure after-action language does not become homework or self-surveillance;
- support any clinical claims about overload, shutdown, meltdown, burnout, and recovery before external review.

Safety flags:

- keep crisis-safety language available where needed;
- avoid method detail;
- do not turn recovery into another performance demand;
- preserve low-capacity alternatives in Move sections;
- maintain anti-fortune-cookie texture through concrete examples.
