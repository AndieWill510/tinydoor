---

## 3. Accepted Findings

### 3.1 The 7-part / 12-chapter / Options model mostly works

Accepted.

The core model remains useful:

```text
44 imported source bins
→ fewer reader-facing parts and chapters
→ many short Options
```

The Option unit still works.

The title earns it.

But the model must be revised before large-scale extraction.

---

### 3.2 Part IV is too large

Accepted.

Part IV currently receives too much conceptual load:

- environment;
- social standards;
- shame cycles;
- mimicry;
- internalized ableism;
- autistiphobia;
- burnout;
- dysregulation;
- regulation;
- self-stigma;
- trauma/cortisol evidence.

This violates the book's own principle:

> A chapter is not a warehouse.

Likely repair:

- split `The Way Home` from `The Body Keeps the Load`;
- treat trauma/cortisol material as evidence support, not a reader-facing chapter.

---

### 3.3 Part VI risks tokenization

Accepted.

One chapter cannot adequately hold eleven different lived-risk surfaces.

Current risk:

```text
one population group
→ one short Option
→ practical insufficiency
→ tokenization by structure
```

Likely repair:

- elevate Part VI into a real part with more than one reader-facing chapter;
- weave extra-load considerations throughout earlier parts;
- avoid making intersectionality an appendix wearing a kinder title.

---

### 3.4 MD is not stable vocabulary

Accepted.

The scaffold currently promotes `MD` too early and too confidently.

Until resolved, do not use `MD` in reader-facing Option titles.

Use provisional neutral labels such as:

```text
suicide risk
self-harm crisis
preventable Autistic death
socially mediated death
community-preventable death
```

No replacement is promoted by this adjudication.

The topic requires safety-style-guide review.

---

### 3.5 IEMS is acronym-heavy and shame-prone

Accepted.

`IEMS` may remain as internal scaffold vocabulary, but reader-facing content must unpack it warmly and immediately.

Do not make a reader decode an acronym before receiving help.

Possible reader-facing phrase:

```text
Autistic First Aid
```

or:

```text
The Four Survival Risks
```

---

### 3.6 "You Are Already Overloaded" needs care

Accepted.

As a title, it can validate.

It can also confirm hopelessness.

Likely repair:

```text
You Noticed Overload in Time
```

or:

```text
Overload Is Information
```

or:

```text
Your Body Is Asking for an Exit
```

---

### 3.7 Mortality enters too early in the current opening path

Accepted with nuance.

Mortality belongs early.

It does not belong before the reader has been seen.

Preferred emotional sequence:

```text
1. body story / saxophone
2. recognition: you were not broken
3. reader promise: another option
4. mortality frame
5. practical survival path
```

Rejected sequence:

```text
welcome
→ pandemic
→ premature death
→ MD acronym
```

---

### 3.8 References and the cortisol paper are not chapters

Accepted.

`420` and `430` should leave the numbered reader-facing chapter path.

They may remain source bins or back matter.

Working status:

```text
420 — Back Matter / Reference Store
430 — Evidence Store / Trauma Physiology Support
```

This may reduce the reader-facing source-bin set from 44 to 42, while preserving the files in `Chapters/` for provenance.

---

### 3.9 PMA is a governing principle, not merely first aid

Accepted.

`Positive Mental Attitude` should be reframed as:

```text
protect access to options under stress
```

It likely belongs as a thread across the book, not only in Part V.

---

### 3.10 Monotropism / Intense World Theory is architecture debt

Accepted.

`290` currently has an unresolved destination:

```text
Part II or IV
```

That is not a plan.

It should be held for scientific-humility review before placement.

---

### 3.11 Sex-positive Autism needs sequencing and safety review

Accepted.

This is not a content rejection.

It is a reader-readiness question.

Before placing sex-positive material early, resolve:

- audience scope;
- age-safety;
- trauma-safety;
- whether the reader is regulated enough to receive it;
- whether the content belongs in main path, later path, or companion material.

---

### 3.12 Autism, Inc. may overpower the survival path

Accepted.

`440` is important.

It may also be a book-sized indictment.

Compress, split, defer, or companion it after determining how much the main reader path can carry.

## 8. Doorway Decision

Decision:

> The saxophone prelude is the first reader-facing doorway.

Rationale:

- it gives the body before the theory;
- it makes the reader feel seen before statistics;
- it carries the broken-tool metaphor;
- it converts shame into recognition;
- it makes the title emotionally believable.

`Chapters/010.md` contains valuable material, especially:

- the welcome;
- the Autistic cliff;
- behavior is not the real problem;
- the shift from behavior to ableism;
- the Blue Zones analogy;
- the phrase `you have another option`.

But it should not be the first reader-facing text as currently written.
