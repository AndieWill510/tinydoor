# Proof, Not Citation Standard

Author: Kevin "Andie" Williams  
Status: Active evidence standard  
Project: Tiny Door / You Have Another Option  
Repository: TinyDoor  
Date: 2026-05-31

---

## 1. Principle

This book is governed by proof, not citation volume.

Working rule:

```text
Not what I can cite.
What I can prove.
```

A citation is not proof by itself.

A reference list is not a finding.

A link dump is not an argument.

A sentence becomes eligible for reader-facing use only when the evidence actually supports the claim being made.

---

## 2. Why This Exists

The project contains earlier reference material drafted during an anger-era phase of the book.

That material matters.

It records where the pain was, where the questions were, and where evidence might be found.

But the purpose of the book has changed.

The book is not now organized around lashing out at ABA, ABA practitioners, parents, clinicians, or institutions.

The book is organized around making harm legible, protecting the witness, building exits, and giving readers another option.

---

## 3. Citation vs. Proof

A source can be cited for many reasons:

```text
background
context
locator
example
community signal
scholarly disagreement
historical artifact
personal research trail
```

But proof requires more.

A source supports a claim only if:

```text
it actually says what the draft says it says;
it is appropriate for the type of claim being made;
it is current enough for the claim;
it is strong enough for the claim;
it is not being stretched beyond its evidence register;
it is not being used to launder authorial anger into factual certainty.
```

---

## 4. Evidence Registers

Claims must be labeled by evidence register before promotion when risk is nontrivial.

Common registers:

```text
memoir
ethical frame
disability-rights critique
clinical claim
public-health claim
historical claim
legal claim
workforce / market claim
community signal
scholarly interpretation
speculation / hypothesis
```

A memoir claim may be true as witnessed without becoming a general clinical claim.

A disability-rights critique may be valid as argument without becoming settled science.

A scholarly interpretation may be useful without becoming undisputed history.

A community survey may be meaningful without functioning as population-level proof.

---

## 5. Reader-Splitting Guardrail

The book should not split readers unnecessarily by turning complex systems into villains too early.

Standing rule:

```text
Do not lash out when discernment will do more work.
```

This does not mean softening harm.

It means naming only what can be supported and letting the reader keep enough room to think.

Avoid:

```text
all ABA is abuse
all practitioners are abusers
all parents are enablers
all institutions are malicious
one study proves the whole system
one story proves every case
```

Prefer:

```text
this goal rewards disappearance
this measure ignores cost
this frame can travel into family life
this practice needs consent and contestability
this source supports this narrower claim
this harm happened here
```

---

## 6. Imported Reference Dumps

Imported reference documents are evidence stores, not governing prose.

They should be marked:

```text
Status: imported / unverified
```

They may be used to seed evidence maps, but they do not authorize reader-facing claims until reviewed.

Workflow:

```text
raw reference source -> normalized index -> evidence map -> claim review -> reader-facing Option
```

No Option should cite or imply a claim merely because a URL appeared in an older reference file.

---

## 7. Chapter 100 Application

Chapter 100 contains scale, workforce, training, jurisdiction, market, and harm claims.

Those claims require an evidence map before reader-facing use.

Possible claim categories:

```text
Autistic population estimates
ABA workforce counts
RBT credential / training requirements
RBT pay
teacher comparison
ABA industry / private equity
jurisdiction claims
harm claims
```

The higher the claim heat, the stronger the evidence requirement.

Especially high-risk claims include:

```text
lethally harmful
scientifically proven to cause illness
millions treated by ABA worldwide
only state-sanctioned intervention
all four groups are culpable
```

These must not travel forward without proof.

---

## 8. Promotion Questions

Before promoting any evidence-bearing Option, ask:

```text
What exactly is the claim?
What kind of claim is it?
What source supports it?
Does the source actually support that exact claim?
Is the claim narrower than the source or wider than the source?
Could a fair critic say we overstated this?
Could this sentence split the reader before it helps them see?
Can the sentence be made truer by becoming narrower?
```

If the answer is uncertain, narrow the claim or hold it.
