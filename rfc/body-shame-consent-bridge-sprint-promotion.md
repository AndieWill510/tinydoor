## 5. What the Sprint Proved

Core arc:

```text
The body was never the problem.
Stigma teaches the body to hide.
Pleasure needs consent, privacy, safety, and care.
```

The sprint establishes:

- Autistic bodies are often corrected before they are understood;
- shame can interfere with body-signal recognition, consent, and safety;
- privacy, boundaries, consent, context, and safety are useful tools, not shame;
- pleasure includes sensory, movement, rest, relational, solitude, and adult lawful consensual sexual pleasure;
- shame should not be allowed to erase safe forms of aliveness;
- pleasure cannot be used to cross another person's boundaries;
- shame cannot be used to erase the reader's boundaries.
