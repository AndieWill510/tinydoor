# Safety Style Guide

Author: Kevin "Andie" Williams  
Status: Draft v0.1  
Project: You Have Another Option  
Repository: AnotherOption  
Date: 2026-05-30

---

## 1. Purpose

This book is about preventing premature inflicted death among Autistic people.

It must speak plainly about danger, despair, preventable harm, coercive normalization, burnout, isolation, institutional failure, and survival.

It must also protect vulnerable readers.

Core rule:

> Do not euphemize the subject into uselessness.
>
> Do not make the vulnerable reader less safe for the sake of a stronger sentence.

---

## 2. Governing Tension

This project lives inside a real tension:

- people can be triggered;
- people also need language strong enough to reach them before the harm becomes final.

The book must not choose one truth and erase the other.

Too much bluntness can overwhelm a vulnerable reader.

Too much softness can abandon the reader who needs the book to tell the truth plainly.

The goal is not maximum softness.

The goal is life-preserving precision.

---

## 3. House Frame

Preferred working language:

```text
premature inflicted death
preventable Autistic death
community-preventable harm
socially mediated mortality
survival margin
harm reduction
another option
```

Under challenge:

```text
man-made death / MD
```

`MD` may remain in source bins for provenance.

Do not promote it into final reader-facing house language until separately adjudicated.

---

## 4. Plain Speech / Safe Speech

The book may name death.

The book may name despair.

The book may name systems that shorten Autistic lives.

But the book should not become vivid in ways that teach harm, romanticize collapse, create doom, or make suffering feel inevitable.

The book's job is to keep a door open.

---

## 5. Reader-at-the-Edge Rule

The book must speak directly to the reader who is close to giving up.

Approved Doorway Pilot sentence:

> If you are reading this from somewhere very dark, you are exactly who I had in mind when I wrote it.

This is reader recognition.

It belongs in the book.

---

## 6. Statistics Rule

Mortality and lifespan statistics require containment.

Do not drop frightening numbers without also giving the reader:

1. context;
2. uncertainty where needed;
3. a reason the number is not destiny;
4. a practical or communal path forward.

Statistics should illuminate preventable harm.

They should not become doom.

---

## 7. Platform / Filter Friction

This project may encounter platform safety filters because it speaks directly about preventing premature inflicted death.

Operational rule:

- do not weaken the book merely to satisfy a filter;
- do not smuggle unsafe content through euphemism;
- when a write is blocked, name the block honestly;
- preserve the authorial decision when the language is safe and necessary;
- keep the work method-safe, reader-safe, and morally clear.

The goal is not to outwit safety systems.

The goal is to keep life-preserving language intact.

---

## 8. Working Sentence Tests

A sentence is likely working if it:

- tells the truth;
- reduces shame;
- avoids instructive harm detail;
- avoids making death feel inevitable;
- does not turn the reader into the problem;
- opens a door;
- gives the reader more room to stay.

A sentence is in trouble if it:

- is cleverer than it is useful;
- makes despair feel like identity;
- implies the reader should fix everything alone;
- uses statistics without containment;
- aims anger at the vulnerable reader instead of the harmful system.
