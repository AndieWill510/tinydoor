# Anti-Fortune-Cookie Audit

Author: Kevin "Andie" Williams  
Status: Editorial control rule  
Project: Tiny Door / You Have Another Option  
Repository: TinyDoor  
Date: 2026-05-31

---

## 1. Purpose

This file records an editorial correction after Andie flagged a real drift risk:

```text
we've devolved into fortune cookie wisdom
```

The concern is valid.

The Option form is powerful, but it can produce beautiful compressed lines before the chapter has earned them.

Tiny Door can have sacred lines.

They must be earned by scene, body, friction, specificity, or usable action.

---

## 2. Problem Pattern

A Tiny Door Option is drifting toward fortune-cookie writing when it:

- ends with a polished aphorism that could be detached from the chapter;
- gives mercy without enough situation;
- offers a Move that is a feeling, not an action;
- replaces evidence with poetic confidence;
- says something true but too general;
- could appear on a wellness mug without sounding out of place;
- makes the reader feel inspired-at rather than helped;
- sounds like the house style performing itself.

---

## 3. Anti-Fortune-Cookie Test

Before promotion, each Option should answer:

```text
What concrete problem does this Option help the reader handle?
```

Then check:

1. Is there a scene, body moment, room, exchange, object, or example?
2. Is the pressure specific enough that a reader can recognize it?
3. Is the reframe earned by what came before it?
4. Does the Move give one usable next action or question?
5. Does the Mercy land after the chapter has done work, not instead of work?
6. Does the line sound like Andie / Tiny Door rather than generic wellness language?
7. Is any scientific or clinical claim being smuggled under poetic authority?
8. Would a reader in crisis feel accompanied, or merely handed a pretty sentence?

---

## 4. Repair Pattern

When an Option feels fortune-cookie-adjacent, repair by adding one or more of:

- a concrete ordinary example;
- a body signal;
- a room / workplace / school / family situation;
- a before-and-after contrast;
- a limitation or caveat;
- a cost;
- a real constraint;
- a smaller Move;
- a less glowing final line.

Do not repair by making the prose more ornate.

Do not add extra aphorisms.

Do not make the chapter longer unless the new material adds texture, friction, or use.


This phrase is intentionally sharp.
