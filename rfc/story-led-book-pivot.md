# Story-Led Book Pivot

Author: Kevin "Andie" Williams  
Status: Editorial pivot  
Project: You Have Another Option  
Repository: AnotherOption  
Date: 2026-05-30

---

## 1. Purpose

This file records the editorial pivot implied by Chris's guidance:

```text
lead / lede with story
```

This does not turn the book into autobiography.

It changes the book's operating method.

The book should lead with story, extract the survival principle, and then give the reader one usable next move.

---

## 2. Core Rule

The author's life is evidence.

The reader's life is the point.

Memoir should function as:

- doorway;
- proof of concept;
- nervous-system handrail;
- anti-abstraction device;
- evidence that the book's claims can become lived.

Memoir should not become:

- the whole book;
- the reader's burden;
- spectacle;
- proof that one person's solution is universal;
- a demand that the reader reproduce Andie's path.

---

## 3. Reader-Facing Pattern

Default Option pattern remains:

```text
Doorway
Pressure
Reframe
Move
Mercy
```

Story-led version:

```text
Doorway: a scene, sentence, image, encounter, or lived moment
Pressure: what the moment reveals about Autistic survival pressure
Reframe: the transferable principle
Move: one small reader action or question
Mercy: the reader is not alone, defective, doomed, or required to solve everything alone
```

---

## 4. Story-to-Principle Rule

Every story-led Option should answer:

```text
What does this story make usable for the reader?
```

If the answer is unclear, the story belongs in Holding or later memoir notes until its reader function is known.

---

## 5. Current Lead Story Function

The Community as Survival Infrastructure arc is the first major proof-of-concept story.

It demonstrates:

```text
community can become survival infrastructure;
stayable life is real;
safety creates ground for becoming;
queer liberation can arrive as ordinary acceptance;
old severed filaments can return;
survival infrastructure can replicate in a new place.
```

But this arc is not the entire book.

It is the lamp that lets the next rooms be entered.

## 7. Guardrails

Do not abandon story.

Do not drown in story.

Do not make the book clinically abstract again.

Do not turn Andie's life into the only path.

Do not explain before the reader has been invited in.

Do not use memoir to avoid evidence, science, design, or public-health framing.

Use story as the door.

Use analysis as the room.

Use the Move as the handhold.

Use Mercy as the reason to keep reading.
